<div class="article-title"><h1>
Devastating Simulations Say Sea Ice Will Be Completely Gone in Arctic Summers by 2050 </h1></div>
<div class="author-name">
<div class="author-name-image"></div>
<div class="author-name-text">
<div class="author-name-name floatstyle">
<span>PETER DOCKRILL </span></div>

</div>
</div>

<div id="item_43965" class="article-mainbody-container">
<div class="article-mainbody-centre">
<div class="article-fulltext">
<p>For<a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/ncomms6608"> millions of years</a>, the Arctic has observed an unbroken ritual. In winter, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arctic_ice_pack">Arctic sea ice</a> expands, as sub-zero polar temperatures freeze waters in their place. In summer, the ice pack retreats, as warmer temperatures thaw the winter-made gains, surrendering them back to the ocean.</p><div class="priad" id='Purch_Y_C_0_1'><script>tmntag.cmd.push(function(){tmntag.adTag('Purch_Y_C_0_1', false);});</script></div>
<div class=priad-mobile-spacer>&nbsp;</div><p>In the era of anthropogenic climate change, the ebb and flow of this timeless cycle has become disturbed. For decades, the overall coverage of Arctic sea ice <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arctic_sea_ice_decline">has been in decline</a>, expanding less and retreating more with each year. Nonetheless, the Arctic has, as always, remained frozen, covered in sea ice, even in the summer.
