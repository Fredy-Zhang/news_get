  <h1>Baby crocodiles at large in Broome after slipping out of Malcolm Douglas Crocodile Park</h1><div class="byline">
      <div class="bylinepromo">
          <a href="/radio/kimberley/">ABC Kimberley</a>
        </div>
      By <a href="/news/erin-parke/4421154" target="_self" title="">Erin Parke</a></div>
  



  
    <p class="published">
      Updated 
    <span class="timestamp">
      April 26, 2020 13:51:08
    </span>
    </p>
  
  

<div class="inline-content photo full">
    <a href="/news/2020-04-26/holding-baby-crocodile/12185070">











  
  
  
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
  <img src="https://www.abc.net.au/news/image/12185070-3x2-700x467.jpg" alt="A baby crocodile with it&#039;s eyes and jaws wide open is held in a man&#039;s hand" title="Holding baby crocodile" width="700" height="467"/>
</a><a href="/news/2020-04-26/holding-baby-crocodile/12185070" class="inline-caption"><strong>
        Photo:</strong>
       One of the crocodile hatchlings found at Cable Beach. Both were given a health check by the staff at Native Animal Rescue Broome. <span class="source">(Supplied: Native Animal Rescue Broome)</span>
      </a></div>
<div class="attached-content">
      <div class="inline-content map left">
      <div class="story-map"></div>
      <a class="inline-caption" href="http://maps.google.com/?q=-17.957,122.2395(Broome%206725)&amp;z=5">
        <strong>Map: </strong>
        Broome 6725</a>
    </div>
  </div>
  <p>Authorities have ordered upgrades to fencing at a crocodile park in Broome after a mass escape of hatchlings, most of which are still on the run.</p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg right">
<div class="inner">
  <h2>Key points:</h2><ul><li>Two out of 11 baby crocodiles that escaped from an old crocodile farm in Broome have been found</li><li>In a separate recent incident, a slightly older 20cm crocodile escaped and was found in a roadside drain</li><li>Parks and Wildlife have ordered fencing be repaired at the old site to prevent further escapes</li></ul></div>
</div><p> </p><p>The baby crocodiles are thought to have slipped through a fence, with two discovered by locals at nearby Cable Beach in mid-April.</p><p>Nine other hatchlings from the same nest remain unaccounted for.</p><p>In a separate incident, a slightly older crocodile â€” around 20 centimetres long â€” escaped its pen and slipped out a gate, and was found in a roadside drain.</p><div class="inline-content photo full">
    <a href="/news/2020-04-26/beach-croc-1/12178076">











  
  
  
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
  <img src="https://www.abc.net.au/news/image/12177834-3x2-700x467.jpg" alt="A small crocodile hides besides a grey rock on a damp beach" title="Beach croc" width="700" height="467"/>
</a><a href="/news/2020-04-26/beach-croc-1/12178076" class="inline-caption"><strong>
        Photo:</strong>
       One of the escaped hatchlings was found hiding in rocks at nearby Cable Beach. <span class="source">(Supplied: Abby Murray)</span>
      </a></div><p> </p><p>Park owner Valerie Douglas said she was installing new fencing along the park perimeter.</p><p>"Everything's under control and we are making changes so it doesn't happen again," she said.</p><p>"The 11 hatchlings escaped from a nest that was missed by staff when they were collecting eggs in January, and some of them got away.</p><blockquote class="quote--pullquote"><p>"Some of the fencing is quite old, and were a bit damaged in a cyclone a couple of years back.</p></blockquote><p>"So we are paying for new fencing to make sure it doesn't happen again."</p><p>She said the reptiles that escaped were not big enough to pose a risk to people or pets.</p><h2>A shock discovery</h2><div class="inline-content photo full">
    <a href="/news/2020-04-26/broome-croc-park-gates-1/12184970">











  
  
  
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
  <img src="https://www.abc.net.au/news/image/12184962-3x2-700x467.jpg" alt="A fence runs in front of a build, covered in weeds and with a sign saying Broome Crocodile Park" title="Broome croc park gates" width="700" height="467"/>
</a><a href="/news/2020-04-26/broome-croc-park-gates-1/12184970" class="inline-caption"><strong>
        Photo:</strong>
       Fencing will be replaced at the old Broome Crocodile Park site, which is closed to the public, but home to hundreds of crocodiles. <span class="source">(ABC Kimberley: Andrew Seabourne)</span>
      </a></div><p> </p><p>The two crocodiles at Cable Beach were found last week by local women going for walks with their children.</p><p>Matilda, whose parents have asked for her surname not to be used, says she spotted the small reptile hiding next to a rock at sunset.</p><p>"When I first saw it I thought it was a lizard, but then I realised it was a crocodile," she said.</p><blockquote class="quote--pullquote"><p>"My mum wanted us all to stand back, and she was looking around for the mother crocodile.</p></blockquote><p>"We stayed with it, because it was getting dark and we didn't want to lose it."</p><p>Both of the baby crocodiles found at the beach have been given a health check and returned to the Malcolm Douglas Crocodile Park, which spans two large properties.</p><h2>Croc parks covered by farming licence</h2><p>The animals had escaped from the 'old' Malcolm Douglas Crocodile Park, which sits on 2 hectares of prime real estate opposite Cable Beach.</p><p>The park was opened by the legendary filmmaker in 1983, as a place for tourists to gather and watch the enormous 'salties' feed on raw chickens and where Mr Douglas oversaw farming for crocodile skins.</p><p>It has been closed to the public since 2012, after Mr Douglas relocated the tourism side of the business to a new site 16km east of Broome and <a href="/news/2010-09-23/croc-man-douglas-killed-in-car-crash/2271372" target="_self" title="">was killed in a freak car accident on the property</a>.</p><div class="inline-content photo full">
    <a href="/news/2020-04-26/old-croc-park-aerial-1/12178356">











  
  
  
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
  <img src="https://www.abc.net.au/news/image/12178078-3x2-700x467.jpg" alt="An aerial view of a lush green park area, with some buildings visible" title="Old croc park aerial" width="700" height="467"/>
</a><a href="/news/2020-04-26/old-croc-park-aerial-1/12178356" class="inline-caption"><strong>
        Photo:</strong>
       The old Broome Crocodile Park is located near Cable Beach in Broome. <span class="source">(ABC Kimberley: Andrew Seabourne)</span>
      </a></div><p> </p><p>There are now about <a href="/news/2019-02-14/biggest-baddest-crocs-of-the-kimberley/10593494" target="_self" title="">2,500 crocodiles across the two sites</a>, which are covered by a crocodile farming licence that requires the animals to be safely secured.</p><p>The Department of Biodiversity and Cultural Attractions said in a statement that the properties were inspected annually.</p><p>"Parks and Wildlife Service is working closely with park owners â€¦ to ensure the park meets the required standards," the statement said.</p><p>"An assessment of the park was undertaken on Friday â€¦ and recommendations were made to the park, which included repairs to sections of the mesh fence."</p><h2>Colourful history</h2><p>It's not the first time crocodiles have escaped or been stolen from the park.</p><p>In 2016, <a href="/news/2016-04-04/broome-diners-shock-as-man-carries-croc-into-cafe/7297442" target="_self" title="">at least six small crocodiles disappeared.</a></p><div class="inline-content photo right">
    <a href="/news/2020-04-26/crocodile-handler-dave-tapper-holds-juvenile-crocodile/12184988">











  
  
  
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
  <img src="https://www.abc.net.au/news/image/7298646-3x4-340x453.jpg" alt="Crocodile handler Dave Tapper holds a juvenile crocodile in his left hand." title="Crocodile handler Dave Tapper holds juvenile crocodile" width="340" height="453"/>
</a><a href="/news/2020-04-26/crocodile-handler-dave-tapper-holds-juvenile-crocodile/12184988" class="inline-caption"><strong>
        Photo:</strong>
       One of the crocodiles that escaped in 2016 was retrieved from a swimming pool at a nearby house by crocodile handler Dave Tapper. <span class="source">(Supplied: Joshua Spong)</span>
      </a></div><p> </p><p>One was found killed by a car in a nearby carpark, and another was found swimming in the backyard pool of a neighbouring property.</p><p>At the time, owner Valerie Douglas said the animals were stolen during a break-in and then set free.</p><p>In October 2019 authorities were called in <a href="/news/2019-08-19/police-investigating-baby-crocodile-theft-broome-park/11428720" target="_self" title="">when a crocodile named Cinnamon went missing</a>.</p><p>The 15-month-old animal had been regularly handled by tourists as part of daily tours, and police were unable to locate him.</p><p>Broome residents are asked to call the local office of the Parks and Wildlife Service if they see a crocodile, so staff can determine if it is an escapee or one that lives in the creek network along the coast.</p>
