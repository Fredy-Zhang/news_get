<p>A total of 874 servicemen in the Russian military have tested positive for the new <a href="https://indianexpress.com/about/coronavirus/">coronavirus</a> since March, Russia&#8217;s Defence Ministry said Sunday. Almost half of them ‚Äî379 people ‚Äî are isolated at home; others are being treated in various medical facilities.</p>
<p>Four people are in grave condition, including one on a ventilator. Russia has so far reported 80,949 confirmed cases of the virus and 747 deaths. The vast majority of the country&#8217;s regions have been on lockdown since late March, with only essential businesses; grocery shops, pharmacies, banks, operating and people ordered to stay at home.</p>
<p>Earlier this month, Russian President Vladimir Putin indefinitely postponed the traditional May 9 military parade marking the 1945 defeat of Nazi Germany in World War II because of the growing outbreak.</p>
<p>Military units have already rehearsed the parade, footage of these rehearsals showed hundreds of servicemen drilling outside Moscow without observing <a href="https://indianexpress.com/article/explained/why-social-distancing-in-data-coronavirus-6313355/">social distancing</a>. Kremlin spokesman Dmitry Peskov said that the military had their own isolation and distancing protocols which allowed them more freedom.</p>
