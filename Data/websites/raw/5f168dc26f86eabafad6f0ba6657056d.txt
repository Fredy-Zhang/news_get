<p style="text-align: justify;"><strong>Kayode Idowu</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Boko Haram has killed five persons in the convoy of a former governor of Borno State, Senator Ali Modu Sheriff, along the Maiduguri-Damaturu highway.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">A security source said the terrorists attacked Sheriff’s convoy around Auno, few kilometres to Maiduguri.</p><div class='code-block code-block-1' style='margin: 8px 8px 8px 0; float: left;'>
<script async src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js"></script>

<script>

</script></div>
<p style="text-align: justify;">The source disclosed that Sheriff was travelling to Maiduguri from Abuja on Thursday evening to take part in the third day post-burial prayer for his father, Galadima Sheriff, who died last week.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">He said Sheriff had travelled in a convoy of nine exotic vehicles.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">The source added that they (Sheriff convoy) were trailed by the Boko Haram around Auno, adding that the terrorists engaged Sheriff’s escort around 6:30 pm on Thursday, and during the crossfire, two police officers, two civilians and a soldier were killed.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">The source said one of the nine vehicles was damaged at Auno as the convoy was rescued by a team of the Rapid Response Squad deployed to Njimtilo to enforce the COVID-19 lockdown.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">The source said the terrorists were pushed back by the team of the Rapid Response Squad.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">It would be the first time the former governor, frequently threatened by Boko Haram, was being engaged by the terrorist group.</p>
