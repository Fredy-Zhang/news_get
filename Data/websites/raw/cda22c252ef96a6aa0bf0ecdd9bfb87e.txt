  <h1>Coronavirus crisis sees Rohingya face growing hostility in Malaysia</h1><div class="byline">
      By <a href="/news/11029686" target="_self" title="">Max Walden</a></div>
  



  
  
    
      <p class="published">
        Posted 
    <span class="timestamp">
      April 27, 2020 04:42:54
    </span>
      </p>
    
  

<div class="inline-content photo full">

  <img src="https://www.abc.net.au/news/image/12185382-3x2-700x467.jpg" alt="Rohingya refugees including children gather and climb onto a truck in Bangladesh." title="Rohingya refugees in Bangladesh" width="700" height="467"/>

<div class="inline-content map left">
      <div class="story-map"></div>

    </div>
  </div>
  <p>Advocates report increased public anger in Malaysia against Rohingya refugees and asylum seekers as the coronavirus crisis deepens, raising concerns of a migrant crisis as South-East Asia experienced in 2015.</p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg right">
<div class="inner">
  <h2>Key points:</h2><ul><li>A series of xenophobic petitions and viral posts have called for Rohingya to "return home" to Myanmar</li><li>Malaysia recently turned back a boat carrying 200 Rohingya, reversing its previous welcoming approach</li><li>The UN and other agencies fear turning away Rohingya could lead to a repeat of 2015's migrant crisis</li></ul></div>
</div><p> </p><p>While Rohingya refugees have historically received popular and political backing in Muslim-majority Malaysia on the basis of Islamic solidarity, anxiety over the COVID-19 pandemic appears to have eroded sympathy for the persecuted group.</p><p>Malaysia had previously allowed <a href="/news/2018-04-04/rohingya-families-of-those-who-fled-to-malaysia-threatened/9619192" target="_self" title="">Rohingya arriving by boat from Bangladesh or Myanmar to enter the country</a>, but last week the Government turned back a boat carrying some 200 Rohingya over COVID-19 concerns.</p><p>Malaysia has confirmed 5,691 cases of the coronavirus including 96 fatalities.</p><p>Some Rohingya are thought to have attended an Islamic gathering in late February, <a href="/news/2020-03-19/coronavirus-spread-from-malaysian-event-to-multiple-countries/12066092" target="_self" title="">which was the source of Malaysia's first major outbreak.</a></p><p>"With COVID-19 we are all afraid," said Sharifah Shakirah, director of the Kuala Lumpur-based Rohingya Women Development Network, a charity.</p><p>"But some, because of misconceptions, are using really, really harsh words against one community, creating hatred against Rohingya."</p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg full">
<div class="inner">
  <a href="/news/2020-04-27/coronavirus-update-australia-covid19-germany-refutes-china-pitt/12187626" target="_self" title="">Coronavirus update: Follow the latest news in our daily wrap.</a></div>
</div><p> </p><h2>'We cannot return home'</h2><p>The mostly-Muslim Rohingya people are the largest stateless population on earth, <a href="/news/2019-12-12/myanmars-leader-says-the-world-has-it-wrong-on-the-rohingya/11791338" target="_self" title="">having fled brutal persecution in Myanmar for decades.</a></p><p>There are more than 100,000 Rohingya in Malaysia, many of whom have lived there for generations.</p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg right">
<div class="inner">
  <h2><a href="/news/2017-09-24/why-are-the-rohingya-stateless/8978966" target="_self" title="">Why are the Rohingya stateless?</a></h2><a href="/news/2017-09-24/why-are-the-rohingya-stateless/8978966" target="_self" title=""><img alt="" class="float--none" src="/cm/lb/8979384/data/rohingya-stateless-explainer-data.jpg"/> </a> <br/><a href="/news/2017-09-24/why-are-the-rohingya-stateless/8978966" target="_self" title="">In Bangladesh they are a burden; in Myanmar they are despised. Where did the Rohingya come from and where do they belong?</a></div>
</div><p> </p><p>"This pandemic has brought out the worst in some with open displays of xenophobia against outsiders who are perceived to be the cause of the virus," wrote Ariane Jeffrey and Thomas Daniel, analysts from think tank the Institute of Strategic and International Studies Malaysia.</p><p>"It is crucial to remember that 'Malaysian first' does not and should not mean throwing the less fortunate non-Malaysians under the bus."</p><p>A majority of neighbouring Singapore's rapidly growing new cases have been <a href="/news/2020-04-25/singapore-hoped-they-contained-coronavirus-but-second-wave-hit/12172446" target="_self" title="">among low-paid, foreign workers who live in crowded dormitories.</a></p><p>Spurred by sensationalist reporting from some local media outlets, many Malaysians have recently taken to social media to claim that Rohingya and other foreigners are not observing the country's lockdown arrangements, known locally as the movement control order.</p><p>One Malaysian anti-Rohingya petition seen by the ABC had gathered almost 20,000 signatures in 24 hours, before being taken down by administrators.</p><div class="inline-content photo full">
    <a href="/news/2020-04-26/sharifah-shakirah-1/12185340">











  
  
  
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
  <img src="https://www.abc.net.au/news/image/12185332-3x2-700x467.jpg" alt="A woman in a hijab stands in front of apartment blocks in Malaysia." title="Sharifah Shakirah" width="700" height="467"/>
</a><a href="/news/2020-04-26/sharifah-shakirah-1/12185340" class="inline-caption"><strong>
        Photo:</strong>
       Rohingya activist Sharifah Shakirah is concerned about growing xenophobia against refugees in Malaysia. <span class="source">(Supplied)</span>
      </a></div><p> </p><p>The Human Rights Commission of Malaysia issued a statement this week, highlighting that refugees and migrant workers, along with people with disabilities and the homeless, were particularly vulnerable due to economic pressures created by COVID-19.</p><p>It called upon the Malaysian Government to do more to support soup kitchens and other initiatives supporting these vulnerable groups.</p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg right">
<div class="inner">
  <h2><a href="/news/2020-04-03/racism-covid-19-coronavirus-outbreak-commissioner-discrimination/12117738" target="_self" title="COVID-19 sees rise in racism complaints">COVID-19 sees rise in racism complaints</a></h2><a href="/news/2020-04-03/racism-covid-19-coronavirus-outbreak-commissioner-discrimination/12117738" target="_self" title="COVID-19 sees rise in racism complaints"><img alt="COVID-19 sees rise in racism complaints" class="float--none" xml:lang="none" src="/cm/lb/12134002/data/racism-teaser-2-data.jpg"/> </a> <br/><a href="/news/2020-04-03/racism-covid-19-coronavirus-outbreak-commissioner-discrimination/12117738" target="_self" title="COVID-19 sees rise in racism complaints">The Australian Human Rights Commission says about one in four people who lodged racial discrimination complaints in the past two months say they were targeted due to COVID-19.</a></div>
</div><p> </p><p>Syaedah, a Rohingya woman who lives with her husband and two children near Kuala Lumpur, arrived in Malaysia in 2000.</p><p>She told the ABC she was shocked by the volume of recent WhatsApp messages and Facebook posts calling for Rohingya people to return to "their country".</p><p>"I have never experienced this kind of thing in 20 years of living here," she said.</p><blockquote class="quote--pullquote"><p>"We respect Malaysia and its people; they have helped us a lot." </p></blockquote><p>The country is also home to around 3 million foreign workers, of whom up to half are thought to be irregular migrants, according to the World Bank.</p><p>A majority of these workers are from Indonesia, Bangladesh, Nepal and the Philippines, and fill Malaysian labour shortages by taking undesirable, low-paid jobs.</p><p>The local chapter of Nahdlatul Ulama, Indonesia's largest Muslim organisation, <a href="https://www.scmp.com/week-asia/people/article/3081263/one-million-indonesian-migrants-malaysia-lack-food-under-lockdown" target="_self" title="">told the South China Morning Post</a> that up to a million Indonesian workers in Malaysia were going hungry because of forced unpaid leave.</p><div class="inline-content photo full">
    <a href="/news/2020-04-27/rohingya-society-penang-1/12186266">











  
  
  
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
  <img src="https://www.abc.net.au/news/image/12186262-3x2-700x467.jpg" alt="Rohingya families look happy as they attend a community event." title="Rohingya Society Penang" width="700" height="467"/>
</a><a href="/news/2020-04-27/rohingya-society-penang-1/12186266" class="inline-caption"><strong>
        Photo:</strong>
       Malaysia's large Rohingya community has established strong roots in the country. <span class="source">(Supplied: International Catholic Migration Mission)</span>
      </a></div><p> </p><p>Ms Shakirah, who grew up in Malaysia and lives in the US, told the ABC that many Malaysians do not differentiate between refugees and migrant workers.</p><p>"Some of them still don't understand why Rohingyas are living in Malaysia, what is happening in Myanmar," she said.</p><blockquote class="quote--pullquote"><p>"People are saying we should go home. They don't understand that the thing with Rohingya is that we cannot return home.</p></blockquote><p>"If we do, we will be killed. We are facing genocide in Myanmar," she said.</p><h2>COVID-19 crisis sees Rohingya turned away</h2><p>Two Malaysian navy vessels escorted a boat carrying 200 Rohingya out of its territorial waters this week.</p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg right">
<div class="inner">
  <h2><a href="/news/2020-04-26/coronavirus-rohingya-covid19-coxs-bazar-refugee-camp/12167524" target="_self" title="Rohingya at risk">Rohingya at risk</a></h2><a href="/news/2020-04-26/coronavirus-rohingya-covid19-coxs-bazar-refugee-camp/12167524" target="_self" title="Rohingya at risk"><img alt="Rohingya at risk" class="float--none" xml:lang="none" src="/cm/lb/12186170/data/rohingya-boy-data.jpg"/> </a> <br/><a href="/news/2020-04-26/coronavirus-rohingya-covid19-coxs-bazar-refugee-camp/12167524" target="_self" title="Rohingya at risk">The main refugee camp in Cox's Bazar, referred to as the "megacamp", is the biggest and most overcrowded in the world. People live shoulder-to-shoulder in makeshift shelters that have less than a metre between them.</a></div>
</div><p> </p><p>In a statement, the air force said that "with their poor settlements and living conditions … it is strongly feared that undocumented migrants who try to enter Malaysia either by land or sea will bring [COVID-19] into the country."</p><p>"Maritime surveillance operations will be intensified," it said.</p><p>This came after <a href="/news/2020-04-17/hundreds-of-rohingya-refugees-rescued-after-months-adrift-at-sea/12155758" target="_self" title="">Bangladeshi authorities rescued a boat carrying 396 Rohingya, at least 32 of whom had died,</a> which had failed to reach Malaysia.</p><p>"They were at sea for about two months and were starving," a Bangladesh coastguard official said.</p><p>Bangladesh's Foreign Minister AK Abdul Momen later declared that "our navy and coastguard are on alert and they have been instructed not to let these boats enter Bangladesh."</p><blockquote class="quote--pullquote"><p>"No more Rohingya will be allowed in," he said.</p></blockquote><div class="inline-content photo full">

But don't take advantage of our kindness," he wrote in a Facebook post.</p><p><a href="/news/2019-04-03/former-malaysian-prime-minister-najib-razak-faces-jail/10797958" target="_self" title="">Despite facing major corruption charges,</a> Mr Najib remains a popular figure among many Malaysians.</p><p>Tengku Emma Zuriana, a volunteer for the European Rohingya Council, this week had an open letter published by a Malaysian news site in which she called for the Malaysian Government to reconsider pushing boats back to sea.</p><p>Ms Zuriana has since received a slew of threats and has been doxed by online trolls, according to Malaysian women's rights watchdog Empower.</p><p>"While these arguments float around that are more than hateful, then people lose their humanity and destroy humanity," said Anwar Ibrahim, a popular figure previously tipped to take over from former prime minister Mahathir Mohamad. </p><blockquote class="quote--pullquote"><p>"As we guard our borders, we cannot let people die."</p></blockquote><div class="inline-content wysiwyg full">
