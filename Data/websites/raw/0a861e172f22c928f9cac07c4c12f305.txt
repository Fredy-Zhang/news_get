  <h1>Coronavirus fears prompt new fines for deliberately coughing, sneezing and spitting on frontline workers in Queensland</h1>



  
    <p class="published">
      Updated 
    <span class="timestamp">
      April 27, 2020 16:20:04
    </span>
    </p>
  
  

<div class="inline-content photo full">

<div class="inline-content map left">
      <div class="story-map"></div>
      <a class="inline-caption" href="http://maps.google.com/?q=-27.4698,153.024(Brisbane%204000)&amp;z=5">

    </div>
  </div>
  <p>People who deliberately spit, sneeze or cough on frontline workers will be fined up to $13,000, the Queensland Government has announced.</p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg right">
<div class="inner">
  <h2>Key points:</h2><ul><li>The fines are part of a new public health emergency order in Queensland</li><li>The State Government says they're designed to protect frontline workers from people deliberately spitting, sneezing or coughing on them</li><li>Queensland currently has 101 active cases of coronavirus</li></ul></div>
</div><p> </p><p>Premier Annastacia Palaszczuk said fines of up to $1,300 could be issued on the spot, while matters that go to court could lead to a $13,000 fine.</p><p>Ms Palaszczuk said introducing the new public health emergency order was "absolutely the right thing to do".</p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg right">
<div class="inner">
  <h2>Queensland COVID-19 snapshot</h2><ul><li>Confirmed cases so far: 1,033</li><li>Deaths: 6</li><li>Patients tested: 99,527</li></ul><a href="https://www.qld.gov.au/health/conditions/health-alerts/coronavirus-covid-19/current-status/current-status-and-contact-tracing-alerts" target="_self" title="">Latest information from Queensland Health</a>.</div>
</div><p> </p><p>She said it would help protect healthcare, retail, food delivery, transport, council and airport workers; health department, police, fire and ambulance officers;  taxi, bus and rideshare drivers and teachers.</p><p>Ms Palaszczuk said it was "disgusting" some people had been taking their frustrations out on essential workers. </p><p>"It's absolutely disgusting to think that some people are putting other peoples' lives at risk," she said.</p><blockquote class="quote--pullquote"><p>"In this environment, can I please ask people to be respectful. Respect is the key here."</p></blockquote><p>Health Minister Steven Miles said the fines were a new public health emergency order "to support our frontline workers".</p><p>"They should certainly never be threatened with being deliberately infected with COVID-19," he said.</p><p>"During a pandemic, a cough can be used as a weapon. A sneeze can be as dangerous as a knife. Spitting is of course always disgusting. </p><p>"And this new order will allow us to protect all of our essential workers from deliberate coughing, sneezing, and spitting."</p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg full">
<div class="inner">
  <a name="remove"> </a><div class="inline-content infographic full">
        <a href="/news/2020-04-10/growth-factor-social-image/12141080">











  
  
  
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
  <img src="/cm/lb/12141080/data/growth-factor-social-image-data.png" alt="Line chart showing Australia&#039;s current Covid-19 growth factor of 1.11 as of April 25 2020" title="coronavirus growth factor embed"  width="340" height="218" />
</a><a href="/news/2020-04-10/growth-factor-social-image/12141080" class="inline-caption"></a></div> <br/><a href="/news/2020-04-10/coronavirus-data-australia-growth-factor-covid-19/12132478" target="_self" title="">Find out more</a><a name="endremove"> </a><a name="growthfactorgraphicEMBED"> </a></div>
</div><p> </p><p>Mr Miles said there had been cases of hospital staff, paramedics, retail staff and nurses being threatened with being coughed upon.<br/> </p><p>"We are all reliant now on those retail workers to supply us with the goods that we need," he said.</p><p>"I want to send a strong signal to the community that those workers should never feel threatened, and I want to send a signal to those workers that we support them, that we care about them."</p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg full">
<div class="inner">
  <h2>What the experts are saying about coronavirus:</h2><ul><li><a href="/news/2020-04-27/coronavirus-critical-juncture-china-the-west-world-order/12179972" target="_self" title="">The coronavirus crisis has revealed what should have been plainly obvious: China and the West have been on a collision course.</a></li><li><a href="/news/2020-04-27/has-new-zealand-done-better-than-australia-on-coronavirus/12182254" target="_self" title="">New Zealand opted for a complete lockdown to eliminate coronavirus. Australia went for a more relaxed suppression strategy. But the two countries' results have been roughly the same.</a></li></ul></div>
</div><p> </p><h2>'We will not hesitate to clamp down again'</h2><p>Mr Miles said overnight the state had recorded three additional cases of COVID-19.</p><p>He said while the result was "very good", it was a reflection of measures put in place two weeks ago.</p><p>To date, Queensland has 101 active COVID-19 cases. Fifteen cases are in hospital and six people in intensive care are being ventilated.</p><p>Yesterday, <a href="/news/2020-04-27/queensland-coronavirus-restrictions-to-ease/12183006" target="_self" title="">the State Government announced from Saturday some restrictions would be lifted</a>, allowing members of the same household to have a picnic, visit national parks and shop for non-essential items.</p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg full">
<div class="inner">
  <h2>Stay up-to-date on the coronavirus outbreak</h2><ul><li><a href="https://abcapp.page.link/download" target="_self" title="">Download the ABC News app and subscribe to our range of news alerts for the latest on how the pandemic is impacting the world</a></li><li>You can also get up-to-date information on the Federal Government's Coronavirus Australia app, available on  the <a href="https://apps.apple.com/au/app/coronavirus-australia/id1503846231" target="_self" title="">App Store</a>, <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=au.gov.health.covid19" target="_self" title="">Google Play</a> and <a href="https://api.whatsapp.com/send?phone=61400253787&amp;text=To%20learn%20more%20about%20COVID-19%20in%20Australia%2c%20press%20the%20send%20button%20%e2%86%92&amp;source=&amp;data=" target="_self" title="">the Government's WhatsApp channel.</a></li></ul></div>
</div><p> </p><p>"We need to see how these easing of restrictions at the weekend [goes] for two weeks, before we will get a sense of whether people are complying with the rules, whether that increased mobility leads to an increased infection rate," Mr Miles said. </p><h2>More Gold Coast beaches open</h2><p>Beaches at The Spit will reopen at midnight Monday while beachfront carparks along the Gold Coast will reopen from this afternoon.</p><p>The Spit, Surfers Paradise and Coolangatta were closed before Easter when large crowds were observed flouting social distancing rules.</p><p>Surfers Paradise and Coolangatta were reopened last week.</p><p>But Gold Coast Mayor Tom Tate said it was not a green light for people outside the city to start heading to the beach.</p><p>"This move primarily is really for locals to be able to come back to the beaches on the Gold Coast," he said.</p><p>Ms Palaszczuk said people should not get complacent. </p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg right">
