    <h1 class="post-title">France to extend coronavirus emergency for two months</h1>
    <p class="article-heading-des">Health minister says emergency will be in place until July 24 and travellers to France will face two-week quarantine.</p>
    <div class="article-heading-author-wrap">
      <div class="article-heading-author-img">
        <div class="article-heading-author-name">
          <div class="article-duration"><time class="timeagofunction" datetime="Sat May 02 2020 17:11:51 GMT+0000">02 May 2020 17:11 GMT</time></div>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="article-readToMe-share-block" id="article-top-share-icon">
      <ul></ul>
    </div>
  </div>
</div></div></div></div></div></div><div></div></div></div></div><div><div><div><div style="width:100%"><div><div class="row article-main-content-wrapper"><div><div><div><div style="width:100%"><div><div class="col-md-8 article-main-content"><div><div style="border-style:none"><article id="main-article-block" class="main-article-block clear-fix"><div class="main-article-body"><figure class="main-article-mediaCaption"><div class="main-article-media"><img class="img-responsive main-article-media-img" src="/mritems/imagecache/mbdxxlarge/mritems/Images/2020/4/23/96dcae23285b4a3faf94dd41372fbbeb_18.jpg" alt="France to extend coronavirus emergency for two months" title="France to extend coronavirus emergency for two months"></div><figcaption>France is one of the European countries most affected by the virus [Bertrand Guay/AFP]</figcaption></figure><div id="inpage-more-on-block" class="inpage-auto-embed hidden-xs"></div>
    <div class="article-p-wrapper" id="body-200771816342556199"><p class="p1"><span class="s1">France will extend a health emergency imposed to fight the new coronavirus pandemic for another two months until July 24, Minister of Health Olivier Veran has said.<br></span></p>
<p class="p1"><span class="s1">A draft law says a lifting this month of the emergency, which began on March 24, would be premature and carry the risk of an intensifying outbreak.Â <br></span></p>
<p class="speakable"><strong>More:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>
<h4><a href="https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2020/04/french-health-official-warns-easing-virus-restrictions-200422161736276.html
?utm_source=website&utm_medium=article_page&utm_campaign=read_more_links">French health official warns against easing virus restrictions</a></h4>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<h4><a href="https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2020/04/french-fishing-industry-fighting-survive-lockdown-200428103314762.html?utm_source=website&utm_medium=article_page&utm_campaign=read_more_links">French fishing industry: Fighting to survive lockdown</a></h4>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<h4><a href="https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2020/04/racism-row-french-doctors-suggest-virus-vaccine-test-africa-200404054304466.html?utm_source=website&utm_medium=article_page&utm_campaign=read_more_links">Racism row as French doctors suggest virus vaccine test in Africa</a></h4>
</li>
</ul>
<p class="p1"><span class="s1">"We are going to have to perform a long-distance run," Veran said on Saturday, adding he was aware that the French people had already been asked for "colossal efforts" in the fight against the virus.</span></p>
<p class="p1"><span class="s1">The bill will go before the Senate on Monday and the National Assembly most probably the day after, said government spokeswoman Sibeth Ndiaye. It is expected to become law by the end of the week.</span></p>
<p class="p1"><span class="s1"><span>The new emergency bill also lays out the quarantine conditions for people coming to France from abroad.</span></span></p>
<p class="p1"><span class="s1"><span>Veran said travellers to France, including French citizens returning home, will face a compulsory two-week quarantine and possible isolation when they arrive in the country.</span> </span></p>
<p class="p1"><span class="s1"><span>"This quarantine will be imposed on any person returning on French soil," Veran told a news briefing after the weekly cabinet meeting.</span></span></p>
<p class="p1"><span class="s1"><span>He said the duration and conditions of quarantine for asymptomatic people and isolation for those showing symptoms of COVID-19, the respiratory disease caused by the new coronavirus, would be defined in a decree to be published.</span></span></p>
<p class="p1"><span class="s1"><span>Decisions to isolate people would be scrutinised by judges to ensure they are justified and fair, he added.</span></span></p>
<p class="p1"><span class="s1"><span>It was not immediately clear whether the quarantine would only apply to people arriving from outside Europe's open-border Schengen area, whether they would need to self-isolate at home or in hotels, and for how long the measures would be in place.</span></span></p>
<p class="p1"><span class="s1">France is one of the European countries most affected by the virus and has reported 24,594 deaths from 167,346 confirmed cases.</span></p>
<p class="p1"><span class="s1">The government has announced the gradual lifting of some lockdown measures from May 11, including the reopening of primary schools.</span></p>
<p class="p1"><span class="s1">Many shops will also reopen and remote-working staff will be able to return to offices as France battles the economic effect of the coronavirus that has already pushed the country into recession.</span></p>
<p class="p1"><span class="s1">In the latest example of the losses involved, the chairman of the national rail operator SNCF said on Saturday his company had already lost two billion euros ($2.2bn) in the crisis, and would probably apply for state aid and cut staff numbers. </span></p></div>
