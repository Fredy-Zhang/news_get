  <p>Australia's coronavirus tracing app, dubbed COVIDSafe, has been released as the nation seeks to contain the spread of the deadly pandemic.</p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg right">
<div class="inner">
  <h2>Key points:</h2><ul><li>The COVIDSafe app will be voluntary for Australians to download</li><li>It traces only contact with other people who have the COVIDSafe app on their phone</li><li>People risk five years in jail for illegally accessing the data collected</li></ul></div>
</div><p> </p><p>Smartphone users can download the app for iPhones and Android and will be able to register their information on Sunday from 6:00pm AEST.</p><p>People who download the app will be asked to supply a name, which can be a pseudonym, their age range, a mobile number and post code.</p><p>Those who download the software will be notified if they have contact with another user who tests positive for coronavirus.</p><p>Prime Minister Scott Morrison has flagged the app as being essential for Australia to be able to ease coronavirus-induced restrictions across the country.</p><p>Using Bluetooth technology, the app "pings" or exchanges a "digital handshake" with another user when they come within 1.5 metres of each other, and then logs this contact and encrypts it.</p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg full">
<div class="inner">
  <a href="/news/2020-04-27/coronavirus-update-australia-covid19-germany-refutes-china-pitt/12187626" target="_self" title="">Coronavirus update: Follow the latest news in our daily wrap.</a></div>
</div><p> </p><p>The data remains encrypted on a user's phone for 21 days, after which it is deleted if they have not been in contact with a confirmed case.</p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg right">
<div class="inner">
  <h2><a href="/news/2020-04-26/coronavirus-tracing-app-covidsafe-australia-covid-19-data/12186068" target="_self" title="">Here's what the COVIDSafe app looks like and what it wants to do</a></h2><a href="/news/2020-04-26/coronavirus-tracing-app-covidsafe-australia-covid-19-data/12186068" target="_self" title=""><img alt="Here's what the COVIDSafe app looks like and what it wants to do" class="float--none" xml:lang="none" src="/cm/lb/12187416/data/covid-app-data.jpg"/> </a> <br/><a href="/news/2020-04-26/coronavirus-tracing-app-covidsafe-australia-covid-19-data/12186068" target="_self" title="">As Australia looks to implement an app that will help ease coronavirus restrictions, privacy concerns remain.</a></div>
</div><p> </p><p>The application will have two stages of consent that people will have to agree to: initially when they download the app so data can be collected, and secondly to release that data on their phone if they are diagnosed with the virus.</p><p>If a person with the app tested positive to COVID-19, and provided they consent to sharing the information, it will be sent to a central server.</p><p>From here, state and territory health authorities can access it and start contacting other people who might have contracted coronavirus.</p><p>Chief Medical Officer Brendan Murphy said the app would provide quicker contact tracing, which was essential for containing the spread of the virus.</p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg full">
<div class="inner">

</a><a href="/news/2020-04-10/growth-factor-social-image/12141080" class="inline-caption"></a></div> <br/><a href="/news/2020-04-10/coronavirus-data-australia-growth-factor-covid-19/12132478" target="_self" title="">Find out more</a><a name="endremove"> </a><a name="growthfactorgraphicEMBED"> </a></div>
</div><p> </p><p>Health Minister Greg Hunt said strict laws would govern the use of information the app collects.</p><p>A user will be unable to access the data on their phone and Commonwealth officials and law enforcement will be unable to access the central server.</p><p>The central server must store all data in Australia, and it cannot be transferred overseas.</p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg right">
<div class="inner">
  <h2><a href="https://www.abc.net.au/radio/programs/coronacast/latest-segments/12025304" target="_self" title="">Coronavirus questions answered</a></h2><a href="https://www.abc.net.au/radio/programs/coronacast/latest-segments/12025304" target="_self" title=""><img alt="Coronavirus questions answered" src="/cm/lb/12027368/data/coronacast-pic-teaser-data.jpg"/> </a> <br/><a href="https://www.abc.net.au/radio/programs/coronacast/latest-segments/12025304" target="_self" title="">Breaking down the latest news and research to understand how the world is living through an epidemic, this is the ABC's Coronacast podcast.</a></div>
</div><p> </p><p>Anyone who accesses the data illegally faces up to five years in jail, <a href="https://www.legislation.gov.au/Details/F2020L00480" target="_self" title="">according to new laws Mr Hunt enacted overnight under the Biosecurity Act.</a> </p><p>He will also seek to legislate the rules for the duration of the pandemic when Parliament returns next month. </p><p>Mr Hunt will also seek an agreement with the states and territories to ensure the information is only used for contact tracing.</p><p>"The safeguards that have been put in place are the strongest ever," he said.</p><p>"Not even a court order can penetrate the law [that prevents the release of the data].</p><p>"Not even a court order during an investigation of an alleged crime would be allowed to be used [to access the data]. </p><p>"So, to the best of their knowledge, there has never been a set of protections like those which have been put in place under law for this app."</p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg full">

</div><p> </p><p>The <a href="https://www.health.gov.au/covidsafe-privacy-policy" target="_self" title="">app is voluntary</a> and it will be illegal to force anyone to download it.</p><p>Mr Hunt unveiled the app alongside the nation's most senior medical officials and lobby groups.</p><div class="inline-content right">

They can also request for any information they have shared with the central server to also be deleted.</p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg full">
