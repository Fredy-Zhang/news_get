<p>SHANGHAI - Shanghai's 130 main tourist attractions have received over 1 million visitors in the first two days of the five-day May Day holiday.</p> 
<p>The scenic sites received 456,000 visitors on Friday and 633,000 more on Saturday, marking a growing travel and leisure demand, according to the Shanghai Municipal Administration of Culture and Tourism.</p> 
<p>The city requires reservations for tours of all tourist attractions to prevent crowding while tourist sites should not exceed 30 percent of their daily or real-time visitor capacity.</p> 
<p>Tourists are also required to wear face masks, show their health QR codes and have their body temperatures taken for their safety.</p> 
<p>"I feel safe and confident with the new reservation measures," said Li Zhi, who has booked tours to the Zhujiajiao ancient town and Shanghai Oriental Land.</p> 
<p>Under the reservation system, tourist sites are also better prepared to prevent crowding and provide better tour experiences to customers, according to Huang Ying with Shanghai Oriental Land.</p>
