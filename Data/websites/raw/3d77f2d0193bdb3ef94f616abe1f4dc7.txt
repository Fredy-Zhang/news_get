    <h1 class="title details-title">B.C. government rolls out massive effort to move homeless from camps in Vancouver and Victoria</h1>
        <div class="details-intro-area clearfix">
            <div class="details-intro">
                Professional movers will work with government, municipalities, health officials and several non-profits beginning this weekend in what will be a massive effort to house people.
            </div>
                <a href="#details-shares" class="details-bubble details-bubble-s scrollto sc" data-url="https://www.vancouverisawesome.com/vancouver-news/bc-government-rolls-out-massive-effort-to-move-homeless-from-camps-in-vancouver-and-victoria-2282623">
                    <span class="sc-s">0</span>
                    <i class="nub1"></i><i class="nub2"></i>
                </a>
        </div>
    <div class="details-byline">
            <time class="timeago" datetime="2020-04-25T17:03:00.0000000Z">Apr 25, 2020 10:03 AM</time>
 By:     <span id="author">Mike Howell</span>

        
    </div>
<div class="captioned details-file" id="file-0"><img alt="oppenheimer" class="caption-img" src="https://www.vmcdn.ca/f/files/via/howe/oppenheimer.jpg;w=960"><div class="caption">The B.C. government announced Saturday a massive effort to house homeless living in Oppenheimer Park. File photo Dan Toulgoet</div></div>    <div id="details-body" class="details-body clear clearfix" data-words="617" itemprop="articleBody">
        The B.C. government rolled out an ambitious and unprecedented plan Saturday to move close to 700 homeless people from camps in Vancouver and Victoria into hotels, motels and community centres.
<p>Professional movers will work with government, municipalities, health officials and several non-profits beginning this weekend in what will be a massive effort to house people.</p>

<p>In Vancouver, one hotel has been identified for those who test positive for COVID-19, although public health officials and politicians continue to say there have been no outbreaks in the camps.</p>

<p>Officials aim to move at least 300 people from Oppenheimer Park in the Downtown Eastside and another 360 living on Pandora Avenue and at Topaz Park in Victoria.</p>

<p>&ldquo;Now, more than ever, with concurrent emergencies of the pandemic and the ongoing opioid crisis, it is time to implement long-term housing solutions that take care of and protect our most vulnerable people,&rdquo; said Shane Simpson, Minister of Social Development and Poverty Reduction, in a news release.</p>

<p>Government expects the majority of campers to agree to move inside, although a ministerial order under the Emergency Program Act set May 9 as the deadline to transition people into housing.</p>

<p>What happens to people <strong><a href="https://www.vancourier.com/news/one-year-later-meet-the-people-still-living-in-oppenheimer-park-1.23977203">who refuse to move</a> </strong>isn&rsquo;t clear.</p>

<p>A total of 686 hotel and community centre spaces have been secured in Vancouver and 324 hotel spaces in Victoria. That includes a floor of a hotel in Vancouver strictly for women. A facility in Victoria is also designated for women.</p>

<p>All accommodations will be monitored by non-profits, which will provide 24-7 support for residents, including access to health care and medication-assisted drug treatment, if needed.</p>

<p>Daily meals and cleaning services will be included.</p>

<p>Staff at each location is expected to monitor people going in and out of buildings to prevent guests from entering. Every person housed must follow isolation protocols and staff will work to ensure all individuals who require self-isolation are doing so.</p>

<p>Government didn&rsquo;t identify the hotels, many of which remain vacant in most cities in B.C. because of travel restrictions, but <em>Glacier Media</em> has earlier reported that Roundhouse and Coal Harbour community centres have opened to homeless people.</p>

<p>Separate accommodation for youth living in encampments is also being arranged through the Ministry of Children and Family Development.</p>

<p>Personal belongings of people in the camps will be put in bins and moved by professional movers dressed in personal protective gear.</p>

<p>Storage for additional belongings will be available.</p>

<p>Cheryl Casimer, a member of the political executive of the First Nations Summit, said in a news release that she appreciated the strategy developed for the Downtown Eastside.</p>

<p>&ldquo;Although the strategy announced today includes a temporary plan to address urgent housing and social service supports needed to curtail the risk of a COVID-19 outbreak in the DTES, it will also allow the province and primary partners to work towards addressing the much needed long-term housing and wellness strategies and needs to support the DTES community,&quot; Casimer said.</p>

<p>Fiona York of the Carnegie Action Project told <em>Glacier Media</em> in an email that &ldquo;making use of vacant hotel rooms is welcome &mdash; but needs to be offered to those most in need rather than simply those most visible.&quot;</p>

<p>Added York: &ldquo;A much greater number of rooms should be made available to all those who need to safely self-isolate, including those who are homeless, in shelters and in SROs with shared washrooms and poor maintenance &mdash; almost 9,000 people are living in inadequate housing in Vancouver alone.&rdquo;</p>

<p>Simpson was expected to hold a news conference this morning with Judy Darcy, Minister of Mental Health and Addictions, and Mike Farnworth, Minister of Public Safety and Solicitor General, to provide further details.</p>
