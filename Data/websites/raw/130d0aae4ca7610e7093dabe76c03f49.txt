            function getCookie(cname) {
              var name = cname + "=";

                var c = ca[i];
                while (c.charAt(0) == ' ') {
                  c = c.substring(1);
                }
                if (c.indexOf(name) == 0) {
                  return decodeURI(c.substring(name.length, c.length));
                }
              }

          <p class="caption text-grey">
            May 4, 2020
          </p>
          <p class="body_copy text-black">ISTANBUL &mdash; The coronavirus pandemic continues to take a heavy toll on global economies and Turkey is no exception. To date, the nation has recorded 127,659&nbsp;confirmed cases of COVID-19 and 3,461 virus-linked deaths.&nbsp;Turkish leaders continue working to stem the spread of the disease with <a href="https://www.dailysabah.com/turkey/new-hope-for-turkey-as-covid-19-recoveries-make-up-more-than-half-of-cases/news">mixed success</a>, as its domestic economic impacts materialize in the latest financial data.

<p class="body_copy text-black">A gauge on manufacturing output in April <a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2020-05-03/turkish-inflation-downswing-is-picking-up-pace-in-shadow-of-lira?sref=4kxkQT8E">fell to its lowest point</a> since the 2008 financial crisis, posing employment challenges for the sector, according to a report published Monday by Istanbul Chamber of Industry and IHS Markit.

<p class="body_copy text-black">The <a href="http://www.markiteconomics.com/Public/Home/PressRelease/1a095f26a39a4a80b2db591d2d3077d1">Purchasing Managers&nbsp;Index</a>,&nbsp;a composite figure of manufacturing performance, dropped from 48.1 in March to 33.4 last month&nbsp;as a devalued Turkish lira increased input costs for companies, with repercussions for new orders and staffing.

<p class="body_copy text-black">The news comes after Turkey&rsquo;s Central Bank lowered its <a href="https://www.tcmb.gov.tr/wps/wcm/connect/e7497d05-15aa-446e-913f-0d51c2f75d4b/RemarksG30_04_2020.pdf?MOD=AJPERES&amp;CACHEID=ROOTWORKSPACE-e7497d05-15aa-446e-913f-0d51c2f75d4b-n7a5nxy">2020 inflation forecast</a> Thursday to 7.4%, down from 8.2% in January, though April figures released today indicate inflation slowed <a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2020-05-03/turkish-inflation-downswing-is-picking-up-pace-in-shadow-of-lira?sref=4kxkQT8E">slightly less than expected</a>. While Turkish regulators have highlighted a record slump in oil prices as a key deflationary trend, rising food prices put upward pressure on overall inflation in the country.

<p class="body_copy text-black">The mixed developments will continue to pose challenges for the Turkish economy as the nation grapples with the impacts of the pandemic, though economic analysts warn current trends may reverse in the coming months due to weak demand and continued strain on the labor market.
        </div>
        
        <div class="break_grid">
    <div class="blue_block sign_up_ad">

        <div class="article_body">
          <p class="body_copy text-black">Atilla Yesilada, a political analyst at Global Source Partners, said he expected the effects of lowered production capacity&nbsp;as result of the pandemic&nbsp;to begin materializing this summer.

<p class="body_copy text-black">&ldquo;The disinflationary forces right now will completely reverse by autumn and we&rsquo;re going to get inflationary forces,&rdquo; Yesilada told Al-Monitor. &ldquo;Of course, they&rsquo;re not going to be massive, but nonetheless, any forecast that predicts a decline in inflation in autumn and winter ought to be taken with some amount of skepticism.&rdquo;

<p class="body_copy text-black"><a href="http://bianet.org/english/economy/223765-annual-inflation-rate-increases-by-10-94-percent">Consumer prices rose 10.94%</a> year-on-year in April, according to the Turkish Statistical Institute, while Turkey&rsquo;s <a href="https://www.reuters.com/article/turkey-economy-trade/turkish-april-trade-tumbles-deficit-widens-13-pct-ministry-idUSL8N2CM1Y5">trade deficit widened 13.4%</a> to $3.4 billion over the same period.

<p class="body_copy text-black">While the Central Bank lowered its inflation forecast amid such trends, Selva Demiralp, a professor of economics at Koc University and director of the Koc University-TUSIAD Economic Research Forum, has repeatedly drawn attention to regulators&rsquo; limited resources in curtailing future economic shocks, particularly as diminished bank reserves continue to be used to bolster the Turkish lira, which recently passed the psychological marker of seven per dollar.

<p class="body_copy text-black">&ldquo;In the short run, the downfall in demand and the fall in oil prices may dominate the upwards pressures coming from the exchange rate,&rdquo; Demiralp wrote in the&nbsp;<a href="https://yetkinreport.com/en/2020/05/01/turkish-inflation-outlook-in-the-aftermath-of-covid-19/">Yetkin Report</a>. &ldquo;In the medium to long run however, I am afraid that the upside risks to the long-term inflation outlook are underestimated.&rdquo;

<p class="body_copy text-black">As many nations face recession amid the coronavirus pandemic, the economic impacts may be felt acutely in Turkey, where a <a href="http://www.metropoll.com.tr/upload/content/files/1849-apr20in5numbers.pdf">survey by Metropoll</a> found 24% of respondents could not meet their <a href="https://www.al-monitor.com/pulse/originals/2020/05/turkey-opposition-coronavirus-istanbul-ankara-local-akp-aid.html">basic needs</a>, such as food and shelter, in April. In the same survey, slightly less than half of respondents said they able to meet basic needs, but nothing more.

<p class="body_copy text-black">Impacts on the labor market remain to be seen, but some analysts are estimating unemployment rates may have hit 20% at the end of April, with further increases expected due to <a href="https://www.al-monitor.com/pulse/originals/2020/05/turkey-mulls-reopening-tourism-coronavirus-economic-crisis.html">lost income from the summer tourism season</a> in Turkey.</p>

<p class="body_copy text-black">Data compiled by Erol Taymaz, an economy professor at the Middle East Technical University in Ankara, suggested non-agricultural labor demand in Turkey could <a href="https://yetkinreport.com/en/2020/04/30/civid-19-and-employment-losses-in-turkey/">decline by 19%-29%</a> as a result of the pandemic, putting 4.2-6.5 million people out of work in Turkey.</p>

<p class="body_copy text-black">Noting the difficult times ahead, Turkish President&nbsp;Recep Tayyip Erdogan announced Monday evening that some&nbsp;<a data-auth="NotApplicable" href="https://www.reuters.com/article/health-coronavirus-turkey-erdogan/erdogan-says-turkey-to-start-easing-coronavirus-restrictions-as-of-monday-idUSI7N29L04F" rel="noopener noreferrer">coronavirus restrictions would be eased</a>. A ban on intercity travel will be lifted in seven provinces and curfews imposed on senior citizens and people under 20 years of age will be eased.&nbsp;</p>

<p class="body_copy text-black">In addition, shopping malls, barber shops and some stores will reopen May 11, while universities will resume courses June 15. The easing comes after four consecutive weekend lockdowns on major urban areas, among other measures, which have reportedly decreased the number of new infections, with Turkish health officials recording a steady decrease in virus-linked deaths over the last week.</p>

<p class="body_copy text-black">The improvements have also caused lawmakers to <a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2020-05-02/turkey-rolls-back-curbs-on-medical-exports-as-outbreak-subsides?sref=4kxkQT8E">lift a ban on medical exports</a> over the weekend&nbsp;as <a href="https://www.al-monitor.com/pulse/originals/2020/04/turkey-aid-covid19-coronavirus-erdogan-satterfield-sweden.html">Turkey continues to send&nbsp;supplies</a> to countries around the world. Still, health experts warn against lifting precautions too swiftly, with the Istanbul Medical Chamber saying in a Monday statement that the pandemic <a href="http://bianet.org/english/health/223796-istanbul-medical-chamber-warns-against-relaxing-covid-19-measures-too-early">remains a serious threat.</a></p>

<p class="body_copy text-black">In the meantime, economic analysts will continue to watch volatility in the Turkish lira&nbsp;as the Central Bank seeks to <a href="https://www.reuters.com/article/us-health-coronavirus-turkey-funding/turkey-held-talks-with-u-s-on-possible-fed-swap-line-turkish-officials-idUSKCN21S1EB">open swap lines</a> with foreign institutions&nbsp;including the US Federal Reserve. The Turkish authorities have maintained they will not seek support from the International Monetary Fund.</p>

<p class="body_copy text-black">Still, Yesilada said a further devaluation of Turkey&rsquo;s currency could have extensive repercussions on Turkey&rsquo;s economy, as companies lacking sufficient cash flows would become more reliant on foreign-denominated loans, which in turn would become more costly to pay off if the lira were to continue its decline.</p>

<p class="body_copy text-black">&ldquo;This week is extremely critical,&rdquo; Yesilada told Al-Monitor. &ldquo;As I watch my screens, there&rsquo;s this bloody battle between foreign traders and Turkish traders over the exchange rate. I don&rsquo;t know who&rsquo;s going to win, but if Turkey loses, then the exchange rate will fly off the handle.&rdquo;</p>

            function getCookie(cname) {
              var name = cname + "=";
              var ca = document.cookie.split(';');
              for(var i = 0; i < ca.length; i++) {
                var c = ca[i];
                while (c.charAt(0) == ' ') {
                  c = c.substring(1);
                }
                if (c.indexOf(name) == 0) {
                  return decodeURI(c.substring(name.length, c.length));
                }
              }
              return "";
            }

      function getCookie(cname) {
          var name = cname + "=";
          var ca = document.cookie.split(';');
          for(var i = 0; i < ca.length; i++) {
              var c = ca[i];
              while (c.charAt(0) == ' ') {
                  c = c.substring(1);
              }
              if (c.indexOf(name) == 0) {
                  return decodeURI(c.substring(name.length, c.length));
              }
          }
          return "";
      }
