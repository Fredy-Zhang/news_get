    <h1 class="hide-for-small-only " itemprop="headline">Bangladesh reports single-day highest 786 new coronavirus cases</h1>

  <div class="section-content clearfix margin-bottom-2">
    <p>Bangladesh today conformed its highest single-day jump of 786 new cases of infection testing 5,711 samples in the last 24 hours.</p>
<p>One more person died from the novel coronavirus during the time.</p>
<p>With this, the death toll from the deadly virus rose to 183 and the number of total infections stood at 10929. </p>
<p>"193 patients recovered from the virus in last 24 hours, increasing the total to 1403," Additional Director General of Health Directorate Professor Dr Nasima Sultana made the disclosure during a virtual briefing today.</p>
<p>Dr Nasima said, "As per the previous guideline, in line with the World Health Organisation, a patient had to take a second test within 14 to 21 days after he or she had tested positive for the virus. If the test result came negative, there would be a third test within 24 hours of the second test. The patient was released from the hospital only if both the second and the third tests result came negative. </p>
<p>However, the new guideline says the patient will be released after he or she recovers clinically and does not have fever, cough and breathing problems."</p>
<p>"Some 6,182 samples were collected and 5,711 were tested in 33 labs across country in last 24 hours," she added.</p>
<p>The authorities placed 128 new individuals suspected of having Covid-19 into isolation, bringing the total to 1,694 after 1,243 left.</p>
<p>"The country is capable of accommodating 3,635 people at 601 isolation wards," Dr Nasima said.</p>
<p>Currently, 41,122 people are quarantined across the country – including 2,477 newly-quarantined individuals. A total of 156,689 people have completed their time under quarantine.</p>
<p>As many as 4,045,060 hotline services were given by 15,622 trained doctors via three designated numbers, including 69,624 in last 24-hour till 8am today. Through mobile phone and websites, 1,695,374 services were provided, including 19,548 new services, added Professor Nasima.</p>
<p>Till date, a total of 679,400 thermal screenings were conducted at different ports, including 188 at airports, 186 at seaports and 173 at land ports in the last 24 hours.</p>
<p>Personal protective equipment (PPE) collected by the government has been 1,930,254 by far, of which 1,549,482 were distributed and 380,772 are left in store.</p>
<p>Globally, the virus has killed 252,566 people and infected 3,658,288 so far.</p>
