        <div class="entry-content read-details">
            <p>The president of Madagascar Andry Rajoelina officially launched a local herbal remedy believed can prevent and cure patients suffering from <span class="caps">COVID</span>-19.</p>
<p>The herbal remedy has been developed by <span class="css-901oao css-16my406 r-1qd0xha r-ad9z0x r-bcqeeo r-qvutc0">Congolese Doctor, Dr. Jerome Munyagi in partnership with </span>the Malagasy Institute of Applied Research and branded <span class="caps">COVID</span> Organics. President Andry Rajaolina presented the remedy to the press on Monday.</p>
<p><span class="caps">COVID</span> Organics contains Artemisia, a plant cultivated on the Big Island to fight against malaria. In other parts of Africa, Artemisia is cultivated in Cameroon, Kenya,Ethiopia, South Africa, Mozambique, Tanzania, Uganda and Zambia &#8211; all in high-altitude regions and/or regions with a pronounced cool period.</p>
<p>All trials and tests have been conducted and its effectiveness in reducing the elimination of symptoms has been proven for the treatment of patients with <span class="caps">COVID</span>-19 in Madagascar, the president said.</p>
<p>The herbal remedy, <span class="caps">COVID</span>-organics is mandatory for children returning to school. President Rajaolina said Monday that it had cured two <span class="caps">COVID</span>-19 cases.</p>
<p>The Covid-Organics will be distributed free of charge to our most vulnerable compatriots and sold at very low prices to others. All profits will be donated to <span class="caps">IMRA</span> to finance scientific research, the president wrote on Twitter.</p>
<p>Im convinced that, in fact history will prove us, but today there are already two cases that have been cured with the Covid-organics, but well actually see what happens next.</p>
<p>Covid Organics will be used in profilaxis, i.e. preventive, but clinical observations have shown a trend towards its effectiveness in curative, other clinical studies are currently underway, he stressed at the launch.</p>
<p>As of May 1st, Madagascars case stats stood at 128 cases of which 92 had recovered with no deaths.</p>
