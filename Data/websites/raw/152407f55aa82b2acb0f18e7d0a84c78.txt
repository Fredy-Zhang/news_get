   <p>Sweden's ambassador to the U.S. believes the country's controversial strategy of imposing only limited restrictions — and not locking down the country — is bearing success, with the capital Stockholm on course to reach herd immunity in the next few weeks.</p>   <p>"About 30% of people in Stockholm have reached a level of immunity," Karin Ulrika Olofsdotter told NPR. "We could reach herd immunity in the capital as early as next month."</p>   <p>Herd immunity means the majority of a given population has become immune to an infectious disease by either recovering from it or through vaccination. Some researchers have put the threshold for <a href="https://www.npr.org/sections/goatsandsoda/2020/03/20/819031274/coronavirus-faqs-can-i-go-running-is-food-shopping-too-risky-whats-herd-immunity">coronavirus herd immunity</a> at 60% <a href="https://www.medrxiv.org/content/10.1101/2020.04.15.20066050v1.full.pdf">in some areas</a>.</p>   <p>There is no scientific proof, however, that people who have recovered from COVID-19 are actually protected from a second infection. <a href="https://www.npr.org/sections/coronavirus-live-updates/2020/04/25/844939777/no-evidence-that-recovered-covid-19-patients-are-immune-who-says">The World Health Organization on Friday</a> said the idea that one-time infection can lead to immunity remains unproven.</p>   <p>Olofsdotter agreed that more research and testing is needed to answer the lingering question regarding immunity. The Swedish government is ready to change its strategy should the situation require it, but there are currently no plans to switch course, she said.</p>   <aside id="ad-backstage-wrap" aria-label="advertisement">
    

    <div id="ad-backstage-News_World_Europe" class="ad-backstage" data-ad-config='{"network":"\/6735\/","site":{"default":"n6735.NPR","mobile":"n6735.NPRMOBILE"},"zone":"News_World_Europe","targets":{"testserver":"false","isPodcastEpisode":"false","storyId":"845211085","agg":["812054919","191676894","434975886"]},"location":"backstage","deferred":false,"isBetweenContent":true,"isAggSponsorship":false,"borderClass":""}'></div>

</aside>   <p>Schools, restaurants and malls have remained open in Sweden. The government has issued social distancing guidelines, discouraged nonessential travel and recommended that people over 70 stay at home. <a href="https://www.government.se/articles/2020/04/strategy-in-response-to-the-covid-19-pandemic/">Authorities also banned</a> gatherings of more than 50 people and visits to nursing homes are prohibited.</p>   <p>While the vast majority of Swedes approve and follow the government's guidelines, <a href="https://www.reuters.com/article/us-health-coronavirus-sweden-stockholm/sweden-to-shut-bars-and-restaurants-that-ignore-coronavirus-restrictions-idUSKCN2262AX">reports suggest</a> Stockholm's residents have begun to break the rules as the weather gets warmer. The government swiftly responded by threatening to shut down any restaurant or bar that fails to implement adequate social distancing.</p>   <p>"I don't want to see any full open-air restaurants in Stockholm or anywhere else. Otherwise, businesses will be closed," Swedish Home Affairs Minister Mikael Damberg said on Friday.</p>   <div id="res845252770" class="bucketwrap internallink insettwocolumn inset2col ">
            <div class="bucket img">

Is Food Shopping Too Risky? What's Herd Immunity?" /></a>         <div class="bucketblock">

   <p>According to the latest <a href="https://coronavirus.jhu.edu/map.html">data from Johns Hopkins University</a>, there are more than 18,500 confirmed coronavirus cases in Sweden, with 2,194 deaths as of Sunday.</p>   <p>These numbers don't bode well when compared to other Nordic countries. Denmark, which earlier this month relaxed some of its restrictions, is closing in on 8,800 coronavirus cases. Its death toll stands at 422. Finland recently extended its ban on mass gatherings through the summer. The country has more than 4,500 confirmed cases and reported 190 coronavirus deaths. Both Denmark and Finland have a population of around 5 million each, which is roughly half of that of Sweden.</p>   <p>"We share the same goal as all other countries, and that is of course to save as many lives as possible and protect public health," Olofsdotter said. "So we face the same reality as everyone else. But what's different, and I think it's important to underline that all countries are different, is that politicians take the measures that they think works best for their country and their general public."</p>   <p>More than half all deaths in Sweden have occurred in elderly care homes, Dr. Anders Tegnell, chief epidemiologist at Sweden's Public Health Agency, <a href="https://www.cnbc.com/2020/04/22/no-lockdown-in-sweden-but-stockholm-could-see-herd-immunity-in-weeks.html">told CNBC on Thursday</a>. The government is investigating the causes behind the high mortality at the nation's nursing homes.</p>   <p>"Once we know how the virus got into our elderly care facilities, the government can make recommendations and take measures to try to stop that, because that is the biggest tragedy of all this, that it has gotten into the nursing homes," Olofsdotter told NPR.</p>   <p>The ambassador said the decision to keep restaurants, shops and schools open during the crisis could potentially speed up Sweden's economic recovery, but she made clear that businesses and employees have been suffering under the pandemic.</p>   <p>"Our unemployment, which was about 6.5% before, is now roughly around 11% and growing," she said. "This is, of course, extremely serious, and we expect that our GDP will shrink between 4-10% for 2020."</p>   <p>European Union leaders last week <a href="https://www.npr.org/sections/coronavirus-live-updates/2020/04/23/843351315/eu-leaders-fail-to-agree-on-coronavirus-economic-recovery-program">failed to reach a deal</a> on an economic recovery program for its 27 member states.</p>   <p>Earlier this month, Swedish Prime Minister Stefan Löfven <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/world/2020/apr/05/sweden-prepares-to-tighten-coronavirus-measures-as-death-toll-climbs">said thousands</a> in the country could die.</p>   <p>"We have chosen a strategy of trying to flatten the curve and not get too dramatic a process, because then the health care system probably will not cope," he told Swedish newspaper <em>Dagens Nyheter</em>. "But it also means that we will have more seriously ill people who need intensive care, we will have significantly more deaths. We will count the dead in thousands."</p>

      <h5 class="hdr">Correction<span class="date"> April 26, 2020</span></h5>
   <p><p>An earlier version of this story said the Swedish government is pursuing a strategy of "herd immunity." Sweden is not pursuing a strategy of herd immunity but rather a strategy of slowing the spread of the virus through imposing only limited restrictions rather than a complete lockdown. </p></p>
