<p>Kim is reported to have sent a thank you message to builders working in the seaside resort of Wonsan.</p>

<p>Rumours have swirled about the <a href="https://www.thesun.co.uk/where/north-korea/">North Korean</a> dictator after it was reported he was either <a href="https://www.thesun.co.uk/news/4203968/kim-jong-un-death/">dead, gravely ill or in a vegetative state</a>.</p>
<p>State-run newspaper Rodong Sinmun has now reported <a href="https://www.thesun.co.uk/who/kim-jong-un/">Kim</a> sent a personal letter to builders working on a tourism project in Wonsan.</p>
<p>Wonsan is also the location where what is believed to be Kim&#x27;s armoured private train was spotted, and where he may be hiding out amid the coronavirus pandemic.</p>
<p>It is unclear if the letter was actually sent by Kim, and no new pictures of the leader have been released by state media.</p><div class="advert-wrapper advert-wrapper--outstream"><div id="outstream" class="dfp-ad advert--outstream"></div></div>
<p>South Korean intelligence has said they are confident that <a href="https://www.thesun.co.uk/news/11483558/kim-jong-un-alive-and-well-wonsan/">Kim is alive and well</a>, but US officials continue to investigate.</p>
<p>Kim was last seen at a meeting of the ruling Workers’ Party to discuss the coronavirus threat on April 11, and is claimed to have undergone heart surgery on April 12.</p>
<p>The North Korean leader was then suspiciously absent from the national birthday celebrations of his late granddad Kim Il-sung, known as the Day of the Sun.</p>
<p>Chinese journalist Shijian Xingzou said a “very solid source” told her the North Korean leader had died.</p><div class="advert-wrapper advert-wrapper--articlempu"><div id="articlempu" class="dfp-ad advert--inarticle"></div></div>

<p>Other unverified rumours state <a href="https://www.thesun.co.uk/news/11487201/kim-jong-un-injured-missile-test/">Kim may have been injured during a recent missile test </a>as there are reports of <a href="https://www.thesun.co.uk/news/11486141/kim-jong-un-death-panic-buying-north-korea-pyongyang/">panic buying in North Korea</a>.</p>
