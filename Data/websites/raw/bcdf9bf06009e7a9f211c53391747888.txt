<p><span>Bishop Panteleimon&rsquo;s remarks come three years after Russia scrapped criminal punishments for first-time domestic abuse offenses and as the coronavirus pandemic has stalled efforts to re-criminalize domestic violence in Russia&rsquo;s legislature.</span></p>																					</div>

											 <p><span>&ldquo;I&rsquo;m asked how to live in obedience to my husband. For starters, stop criticizing [him],&rdquo; Bishop Panteleimon, the head of the church&rsquo;s department for charity, </span><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r9RgZIjNO5k"><span>said</span></a><span> in a live YouTube chat Wednesday.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span>&ldquo;Promise this to God: if I criticize, then I&rsquo;ll make 10 bows in the evening ... or I won&rsquo;t eat chocolate or surf the internet all day,&rdquo; Bishop Panteleimon said.</span></p>
<p><span>On Sunday, Bishop Panteleimon accused the media of &ldquo;biased&rdquo; coverage and </span><a href="http://www.diaconia.ru/v-usloviyakh-izolyacii-osobenno-vazhno-sokhranyat-mir-v-seme-episkop-panteleimon"><span>explained</span></a><span> that &ldquo;it&rsquo;s equally important for husbands to stop admonishing their wives.&rdquo;</span></p>
<p><span>&ldquo;If a husband and wife can&rsquo;t restrain themselves&hellip; they can make a personal commitment to give up what they like for a while,&rdquo; he said in a statement on his Church department&rsquo;s website.</span></p>
<p><span>&ldquo;We all need to understand the other person&hellip; Our remarks to each other can provoke tension in relationships. It's important to avoid this,&rdquo; Bishop Panteleimon said.</span></p>
<p><span>In March, the government of Malaysia <a href="https://edition.cnn.com/2020/04/02/asia/malaysia-womens-advice-controversy-coronavirus-intl-scli-hnk/index.html" title="apologized">apologized</a> for advising women to &ldquo;avoid nagging&rdquo; their husbands </span><span>during the country's Covid-19 lockdown.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span>Other tips issued by the Malaysian women&rsquo;s ministry online urged women to wear make-up and dress neatly, sparking anger and mockery on social media.</span></p> 																					</div>
