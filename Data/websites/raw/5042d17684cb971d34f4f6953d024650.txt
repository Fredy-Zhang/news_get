sics-component__story__paragraph">For the first time in seven weeks, New Zealand has <a href="https://www.stuff.co.nz/national/health/coronavirus/121394819/coronavirus-no-new-covid19-cases-deaths">gone 24 hours</a> with no new cases of <a href="http://www.stuff.co.nz/national/health/coronavirus?rm=m">coronavirus</a>.&nbsp;</p><p class="sics-component__html-injector sics-component__story__paragraph"> At its peak, health authorities saw new daily case numbers in the 60s, 70s, and 80s&nbsp;– hitting 89, the highest daily total, twice.&nbsp;</p><p class="sics-component__html-injector sics-component__story__paragraph"> The past fortnight had seen numbers drop largely day-on-day&nbsp;to just smatterings in the single digits, before today hitting zero.</p><p class="sics-component__html-injector sics-component__story__paragraph"> But what does this actually mean? We ask the experts.&nbsp;</p><div id="fourth-par-video" class="sics-component__oovvuu-recommendation"></div><p class="sics-component__html-injector sics-component__story__paragraph"> <strong>READ MORE:<br> *&nbsp;<a href="https://www.stuff.co.nz/national/health/coronavirus?rm=m">Coronavirus: Full coverage</a></strong><br> <strong>* <a href="https://www.stuff.co.nz/national/health/coronavirus/121394819/coronavirus-no-new-covid19-cases-deaths">No new Covid-19 cases, deaths</a></strong><br> <strong>* <a href="https://www.stuff.co.nz/national/health/coronavirus/121147156/coronavirus-contact-tracing-system-blamed-for-new-zealand-remaining-in-covid19-lockdown?rm=a">Coronavirus: Contact tracing system blamed for New Zealand remaining in Covid-19 lockdown</a><br> * <a href="https://www.stuff.co.nz/national/health/coronavirus/121067048/coronavirus-why-covid19-alert-level-3-could-be-almost-as-psychologically-tough-as-level-4?rm=a">Coronavirus: Why Covid-19 alert level 3 could be almost as psychologically tough as level 4</a></strong></p><p class="sics-component__html-injector sics-component__story__paragraph"> Dr Ashley Bloomfield said the figures are encouraging, but the key test will come later this week.</p><span></span><p class="sics-component__html-injector sics-component__story__paragraph"> That is when new cases that have emerged since moving to level 3 will likely become symptomatic.</p><figure class="sics-component__story-image"><div class="sics-component__story-image__image-wrapper"><img class="lazyimg" alt="On Thursday, director-general of health Dr Ashley Bloomfield explained that at some point, almost all of the country&#x27;s cases were imported. While the ministry&#x27;s capacity to contact trace had increased, there were still some unknowns.</div></figcaption></figure><p class="sics-component__html-injector sics-component__story__paragraph"> Infectious disease physician Dr Ayesha Verrall said the milestone is something to be "optimistic" about, but also a sign there's still work to be done.</p><p class="sics-component__html-injector sics-component__story__paragraph"> The indicator we should be looking at the most is&nbsp;<a href="https://www.health.govt.nz/our-work/diseases-and-conditions/covid-19-novel-coronavirus/covid-19-current-situation/covid-19-current-cases#transmission">locally acquired cases</a> –&nbsp;contacts of a known case, and very rarely community transmission,&nbsp;Verrall said.&nbsp;</p><p class="sics-component__html-injector sics-component__story__paragraph"> These are all new cases which are not imported from overseas,&nbsp;and therefore are transmitted among the community.&nbsp;</p><p class="sics-component__html-injector sics-component__story__paragraph"> Even if we have zero locally acquired cases, the likelihood is we will continue to see new cases imported from overseas, she said.</p><span><div class="sics-component__html-asset sics-component__html-asset--no-scale"><div id="html-asset__120004731" name="120004731" width="100%"></div></div></span><p class="sics-component__html-injector sics-component__story__paragraph"> This will likely continue as long as the virus remains&nbsp;uncontained&nbsp;overseas.&nbsp;</p><p class="sics-component__html-injector sics-component__story__paragraph"> "When you get to several days of zero locally acquired cases, that's pretty cool. I would be pretty excited about that," she said.&nbsp;</p><p class="sics-component__html-injector sics-component__story__paragraph"> While it was "reassuring" to have zero new cases, we can't abandon our efforts.&nbsp;</p><p class="sics-component__html-injector sics-component__story__paragraph"> Verrall said the conversation around "elimination"&nbsp;and the criteria of that is still ongoing.&nbsp;</p><p class="sics-component__html-injector sics-component__story__paragraph"> But if New Zealand is to be certified as having eliminated Covid-19, this might mean activities that weren't possible before are now possible again, she said</p><p class="sics-component__html-injector sics-component__story__paragraph"> "Elimination is not zero cases, but zero tolerance," she said.&nbsp;</p><p class="sics-component__html-injector sics-component__story__paragraph"> It is important to still be "going hard" on testing and&nbsp;contact tracing, and for border controls to remain in place.&nbsp;</p><p class="sics-component__html-injector sics-component__story__paragraph"> Time spent with no new cases –&nbsp;over a number of days –&nbsp;is also crucial, she said.&nbsp;</p><figure class="sics-component__story-image"><div class="sics-component__story-image__image-wrapper"><img class="lazyimg" alt="Dr Ayesha Verrall says she&#x27;ll be &quot;excited&quot; world, she said.&nbsp;</p><p class="sics-component__html-injector sics-component__story__paragraph"> This is because of&nbsp;widespread testing, and relatively low burden&nbsp;on the healthcare system – meaning "we can be out there looking for the needle in the haystack".&nbsp;</p><p class="sics-component__html-injector sics-component__story__paragraph"> To work towards elimination, it's critical that people with any symptoms are tested early,&nbsp;giving authorities the power to more quickly catch up with people, she said.</p><p class="sics-component__html-injector
