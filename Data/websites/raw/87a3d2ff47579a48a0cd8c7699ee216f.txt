										<p><em><strong>Zimbabwe Minister Taunts Chinese</strong></em></p>
<figure id="attachment_147395" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-147395" style="width: 728px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img class="wp-image-147395 size-full" src="https://cy4ad5.c2.acecdn.net/wp-content/uploads/2019/09/Energy-Mutodi_01.jpg" alt="Zimbabwe Minister Taunts Chinese" width="728" height="512" srcset="https://cy4ad5.c2.acecdn.net/wp-content/uploads/2019/09/Energy-Mutodi_01.jpg 728w, https://cy4ad5.c2.acecdn.net/wp-content/uploads/2019/09/Energy-Mutodi_01-300x211.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 728px) 100vw, 728px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-147395" class="wp-caption-text">Energy Mutodi</figcaption></figure>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>A¬† Zimbabwe Minister has sparked controversy after offering to export beef to China, because he wants the Chinese to stop eating &#8220;dogs, cats, snakes, bats, monkeys, baboons and other wildlife&#8221;.</p>
<p>Energy Mutodi who is a member of the ruling Zanu PF party and the Deputy Minister of Information, Publicity and Broadcasting Services said that the consumption of wildlife in Asia and some parts of Africa is the reason why humans are contracting diseases such as the coronavirus (Covid-19) and Ebola. Mutodi then cheekily said that Zimbabwe was hoping to export huge amounts of beef to China, Indonesia and other Asian countries in an effort to stop this.</p>
<p>To buttress his point, the minister shared a video from Aljazeera News which showed a wet market in China where things like snakes, dogs and some other wildlife are being openly displayed for sale. In the video, the World Health Organisation (WHO) is calling on Asian countries to institute stricter standards for safety and hygiene when the wet markets reopen.</p>
<p>Writing on social media platform Twitter, Mutodi <a href="https://twitter.com/energymutodi/status/1253779434290757633" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">said</a>,</p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Mutodi who is fast building a reputation as a garrulous motormouth seems to be under the impression that the consumption of wildlife and other domestic animals is down to the lack of beef.</p>
<p>Many Zimbabwean social media users quickly schooled the minister over his reckless and misinformed taunting of the Chinese. Many warned that he was insulting China, the one country the Zimbabwe government claims to be &#8216;all-weather&#8217; friends. However, it seems that Mutodi was not moved. Despite the avalanche of responses to his post, the Minister did not delete his tweet or attempt to spin it in any way.</p>
<p>The Chinese Embassy in Zimbabwe was yet to respond to the Minister&#8217;s position at the time of publishing. However, barely 24 hours earlier, Chinese deputy ambassador to Zimbabwe, Zhao Baogang attracted a lot of criticism on Twiter when he appeared to celebrate the <a href="https://iharare.com/zimbabwean-nurses-in-the-uk-speak-out-on-devastating-toll-as-more-than-29-die-of-coronavirus/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">deaths of Zimbabwe health care workers who succumbed to the coronavirus in the United Kingdom (UK).</a></p>
<p>In the tweet which has since been deleted Zhao seemed happy that Zimbabweans were dying in the UK because it <a href="https://iharare.com/chinese-ambassador-shades-uk/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">underlined how well the Chinese are apparently treating Africans in their country</a>. Zhao&#8217;s tweet seems to have been a response to reports that Africans in China were being racially abused and inhumanely treated by locals who accuse them of having the coronavirus.</p>
