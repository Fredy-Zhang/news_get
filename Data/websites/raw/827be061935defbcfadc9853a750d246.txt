  <p>West Australian Premier Mark McGowan has announced a "cautious relaxation" of some COVID-19 restrictions in the State, including allowing indoor and outdoor non-work gatherings of up to 10 people.</p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg right">
<div class="inner">
  <h2>Key Points:</h2><ul><li>Gatherings of up to 10 people — indoor and outdoor — will be permitted</li><li>People are advised to stay home except for some key activities, including work</li><li>Non-contact recreational activities, including picnics and fishing, are allowed</li></ul></div>
</div><p> </p><p>Mr McGowan says this will "ensure family and friends can stay connected during the pandemic."</p><p>"The changes announced today are sensible and reasonable and are designed to provide some relief for Western Australians," Mr McGowan said.</p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg right">
<div class="inner">
  <h2>WA COVID-19 snapshot</h2><ul><li>Confirmed cases so far: 549</li><li>Deaths: 8</li><li>Tested negative: 34,842</li></ul><a href="https://ww2.health.wa.gov.au/Articles/A_E/Coronavirus/COVID19-statistics" target="_self" title="">Latest information from the </a><a href="https://ww2.health.wa.gov.au/Articles/A_E/Coronavirus/COVID19-statistics" target="_self" title="">WA Health Department</a><br/> </div>
</div><p> </p><p>In WA, a maximum of 10 people will now be allowed at weddings, an increase from the five-person limit.</p><p>The 10-person limit remains for funerals.</p><p>By increasing the gathering restriction to 10 people, the Government is also trying to trigger economic activity in the housing sector.</p><p>Currently, home opens can only occur by appointment. But from tomorrow, home opens and display village openings will be allowed, under strict health controls.</p><p>The Government's advice is for people to stay home except for shopping for food and other necessary supplies; medical or other health care needs (including compassionate requirements); exercise (including outdoor personal training without shared equipment); training or university study where remote learning is unavailable; childcare or school; and going to work.</p><p>Western Australians can now also leave home for non-contact recreational activities, such as private picnics in the park, fishing, boating, hiking or camping.</p><p>WA is now aligned with South Australia on the 10-person rule.</p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg full">
<div class="inner">
  <a href="/news/2020-04-27/coronavirus-update-australia-covid19-germany-refutes-china-pitt/12187626" target="_self" title="">Coronavirus update: Follow the latest news in our daily wrap.</a></div>
</div><p> </p><div class="inline-content photo full">

</a><a href="/news/2020-04-26/kings-park-1/12187336" class="inline-caption"><strong>
        Photo:</strong>
       Non-essential gatherings in Perth will be relaxed to a maximum of 10 people. <span class="source">(ABC News: Keane Bourke)</span>
      </a></div><p> </p><h2>Playgrounds, skateparks remain shut</h2><p>There were no new COVID-19 cases recorded in the state over the last day, meaning the total remains at 549.</p><p>Mr McGowan said WA had been successful in curbing the spread of COVID-19.</p><p>"So we can relax some of these restrictions, cautiously," he said.</p><p>But many other restrictions remain in place.</p><p>All public playgrounds, skateparks and outdoor gym equipment will remain closed.</p><p>Restaurants, cafes, food vans and food courts remain restricted to takeaway and home delivery.</p><h2>Public transport back on track</h2><p>Perth trains and buses will return to their normal timetables within the next week.</p><p>Public transport services had been reduced due to the coronavirus pandemic.</p><p>But the Government said with schools starting again it wanted services running to a normal schedule as soon as possible.</p><div class="inline-content photo full">
    <a href="/news/2020-04-26/perth-train-in-guildford-1/12187332">











  
  
  
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
  <img src="https://www.abc.net.au/news/image/12187304-3x2-700x467.jpg" alt="A passing train crosses a pedestrian walkway" title="Perth train" width="700" height="467"/>
</a><a href="/news/2020-04-26/perth-train-in-guildford-1/12187332" class="inline-caption"><strong>
        Photo:</strong>
       Public transport services in Perth are set to resume to normal schedules earlier than expected. <span class="source">(ABC News: Gian De Poloni)</span>
      </a></div><p> </p><p>From Wednesday, bus services would be back at pre-pandemic levels, including school buses.</p><p>Regional school bus services would also start again on Wednesday.</p><p>Full train services would return to normal on May 4.</p><p>Parking at train stations would remain free and late-night weekend trains would still be cancelled.</p><h2>Premier says economy is 'dire'</h2><p>The Premier acknowledged the economic impact of the restrictions had been devastating.</p><p>"Our economic situation remains dire," he said, adding the state and federal governments continued to do what they could "to help us get to the other side of this pandemic."</p><p>Mr McGowan warned new cases would continue in WA over coming weeks, which was why the state's borders would remain shut "for a long period of time".</p><p>"The changes announced today are sensible and reasonable and are designed to provide some relief for West Australians," Mr McGowan said.</p><p>"They are low risk but deliver high social benefits. West Australians have done such a great job so far. Although these changes are small, I hope they will be of some benefit."</p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg full">
