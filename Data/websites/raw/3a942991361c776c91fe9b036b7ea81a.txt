against.</p><p>About 1,000 people turned out for the rally, which has become a weekly event in the German capital.</p><p>Saturday's protest attracted mainly far-left activists but there were also right-wing supporters and members of other fringe groups.</p><p>Police put up barriers around Rosa Luxemburg square, where the protesters were headed, leaving the participants to gather in nearby roads.</p><p><div class="embed embed--image"><img src="https://sl.sbs.com.au/public/image/file/4eb1d1ca-32eb-47af-89b4-dbe20f4e2eb3"  alt="German riot police officers in Berlin escort a demonstrator life" while others simply called for "freedom".</p><p>Others brandished placards bearing slogans such as "Stop the pharmaceutical lobby".</p><p>The protest was unauthorised as a result of emergency rules in Berlin banning gatherings of more than 20 people.</p><p><div class="block__container"><div class="label">READ MORE</div><div class="preview__list-wrapper"><div class="preview discontent with the confinement rules has been growing gradually in Germany, as in other countries, though Merkel's popularity remains high.</p><p>She has received plaudits for her management of the health crisis which has seen Germany's COVID-19 toll - 5,500 according to an AFP tally - remain significantly lower than in Italy, Spain, France and Britain where the death tolls have all risen above 20,000.</p><p><div class="block__container"><div class="label">READ MORE</div><div class="preview__list-wrapper"><div class="preview preview--linear"><div class="preview__wrap"><div class="media-image
