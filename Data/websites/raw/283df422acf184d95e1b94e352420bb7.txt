<p>One former North Korean dissident and diplomat <strong>apologized for loudly claiming that North Korea’s Kim was so ill that he couldn't even stand up</strong>, awkwardly issued shortly ahead of the leader contradicting such claims in appearing publicly at a Sunchon Phosphatic Fertilizer Factory on Saturday, attended by hundreds of officials.</p>

<p>The Guardian <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/world/2020/may/04/north-korean-defectors-sorry-after-false-kim-jong-un-speculation">reports that</a> "High-profile defectors from the country speculated that Kim was suffering from a grave illness or could even be dead."</p>

<p>Notably among these is Thae Yong-ho, former deputy ambassador to Britain and one of two defectors elected to the South Korean parliament last month. He was forced to issue a public apology on Monday, <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/world/2020/may/04/north-korean-defectors-sorry-after-false-kim-jong-un-speculation">saying</a> "I am aware that one of the reasons why many of you voted for me as a lawmaker is with the expectations of an accurate analysis and projections on North Korean issues," in a statement. <strong>"I feel the blame and heavy responsibility."</strong></p>

<p>"Whatever the reasons, I apologize to everyone," he added.</p>

<p>And another prominent North Korean dissident who was so far off base that he's since had to fast publicly back-track is Ji Seung-ho, also recently elected to the South's parliament, who previously said in a media interview he's <strong>99% sure that Kim died after cardiovascular surgery</strong>.</p>

<p>Ji Seung-ho had even said Pyongyang was preparing to make the major announcement to the world of Kim's "death" on Saturday <em>— </em>instead the world finally saw Kim in the flesh, <a href="https://www.zerohedge.com/geopolitical/ashy-bloated-analysts-pore-over-kim-jong-un-photos-clues-mystery-absence">ashy and bloated</a> though it was. </p>

<blockquote>
<p>“I have pondered on myself for the past few days, and felt the weight of the position that I’m in,” Ji <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/world/2020/may/04/north-korean-defectors-sorry-after-false-kim-jong-un-speculation">said in a statement</a>. “As a public figure, I will behave carefully going forward.“</p>

<p><strong>Ji had told Reuters on Friday he had received information about Kim’s death from a source he could not disclose.</strong></p>
</blockquote>

<p>The false predictions from what's essentially the leadership of a 'political opposition in exile' community in South Korea set off a firestorm of criticism in Seoul.</p>

<p>The ruling Democratic Party of South Korea issued a statement condemning the prominent dissidents and newly elected lawmakers' words, <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/world/2020/may/04/north-korean-defectors-sorry-after-false-kim-jong-un-speculation">saying the</a> <strong>“repercussions could have been more serious than merely misleading the public.”</strong></p>

<p>And one of the party’s members went so far as to demand their removal from the Intelligence and Defense committees in Parliament.</p>
