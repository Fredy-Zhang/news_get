  <p>The NSW Health Minister has expressed his disappointment in people who flouted social distancing rules on Sydney beaches after council relaxed the lockdown for their benefit.</p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg right">
<div class="inner">
  <h2>Key points:</h2><ul><li>There were 12 new coronavirus cases across NSW</li><li>Testing rates fell to 4,840 compared to 7,352 the previous day</li><li>An employee at an aged care facility in the Blue Mountains has tested positive</li></ul></div>
</div><p> </p><p>There was a slight rise in coronavirus infections in NSW between Friday and Saturday with 12 new cases, including four at the Anglicare Newmarch House aged care facility in western Sydney, where a sixth resident died on Saturday morning.</p><p>The 83-year-old man had tested positive to COVID-19 and had multiple health issues, Anglicare said in a statement.</p><p>Of the four new infections at the facility, two were residents and two were staff, bringing the total number of cases at Newmarch House to 48 (31 residents, 17 staff).</p><p>An outbreak of coronavirus was unintentionally started there by a <a href="/news/2020-04-15/coronavirus-cases-in-nsw-steady-amid-outbreak-at-newmarch-house/12148506" target="_self" title="">staff member who worked six shifts before testing positive for COVID-19.</a></p><p>There is now a total of 2,994 coronavirus cases in NSW.</p><h2>'Disappointment and agitation' over beach behaviour</h2><p>This morning, swathes of people descended on Randwick beaches after the council decided on Friday to close the beaches again, apart from a three-hour window between 6:00am to 9:00am.</p><div class="inline-content photo full">
    <a href="/news/2020-04-25/coogee-on-saturday/12185068">

       Coogee beach in Sydney's east on Saturday morning.  <span class="source">(Twitter: @brucetempleton)</span>
      </a></div><p> </p><p>Coogee, Clovelly and Maroubra were shut early on Friday after people failed to adhere to the rules that the beaches were to be used for exercise only.</p><p>Health Minister Brad Hazzard said it was worrying that people still could not be trusted.</p><p>"I have to express a degree of disappointment and agitation about the fact that some people, when the rules are relaxed, when we try and do the right thing by giving people the opportunity to have some outside exercise, [they] are disregarding the very strong message of social distancing," he said.</p><div class="media-wrapper-dl inline-content video full" data-uri="coremedia://video/12184924">
