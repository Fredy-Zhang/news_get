    <div class="article__body">
      <p>Australia's coronavirus tracing app, dubbed COVIDSafe, has been released as the nation seeks to contain the spread of the deadly pandemic.</p>
<div class="photo-captioned photo-captioned-full photo-cntr eight_col " itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
<img loading="lazy" src="/assets/news/227558/eight_col_000_1Q18BX.jpg?1586371203" width="720" height="480" alt="Government Technology Agency (GovTech) staff demonstrate Singapore's new contact-tracing smarthphone app called TraceTogether, as a preventive measure against the COVID-19 coronavirus in Singapore .">
<p class="photo-captioned__information">
<span itemprop="caption" class="caption">File photo. </span>
<span class="credit">Photo: <span itemprop="copyrightHolder">AFP</span></span>
</p>
</div>
<p>Smartphone users can download the app for iPhones and Android but will be unable to register their information until after 6:00pm AEST.</p>
<p>People who download the app will be asked to supply a name, which can be a pseudonym, their age range, a mobile number and post code.</p>
<p>Those who download the software will be notified if they have contact with another user who tests positive for coronavirus.</p>
<p>Prime Minister Scott Morrison has flagged the app as being essential for Australia to be able to ease coronavirus-induced restrictions across the country.</p>
<p>Using Bluetooth technology, the app "pings" or exchanges a "digital handshake" with another user when they come within 1.5 metres of each other, and then logs this contact and encrypts it.</p>
<p>The data remains encrypted on a user's phone for 21 days, after which it is deleted if they have not been in contact with a confirmed case.</p>
<p>The application will have two stages of consent that people will have to agree to: initially when they download the app so data can be collected, and secondly to release that data on their phone if they are diagnosed with the virus.</p>
<p>If a person with the app tested positive for Covid-19, and provided they consent to sharing the information, it will be sent to a central server.</p>
<p>From here, state and territory health authorities can access it and start contacting other people who might have contracted coronavirus.</p>
<p>Chief Medical Officer Brendan Murphy said the app would provide quicker contact tracing, which was essential for containing the spread of the virus.</p>
<p>Health Minister Greg Hunt said strict laws would govern the use of information the app collects.</p>
<p>A user will be unable to access the data on their phone and Commonwealth officials and law enforcement will be unable to access the central server.</p>
<p>The central server must store all data in Australia, and it cannot be transferred overseas.</p>
<p>Anyone who accesses the data illegally faces up to five years in jail.</p>
<p><b><i>- ABC</i></b></p>
