<div class="comp-rich-text article-text clearfix" id="comp-rich-text12" itemprop=text >
<p>Australia's largest flour mill is increasing its production capacity, allowing it to produce an extra 50 million kilograms of flour by the middle of the year.</p><p>The huge increase in the production of flour is because of the coronavirus pandemic and consumer demand. </p><div class="comp-embedded-float-right">
<div class="view-wysiwyg" >
<h2 class="title collectTitle">
Flour key points
</h2> <div class="comp-rich-text clearfix" id="comp-rich-text13" >
<h2>Key points:</h2><ul><li>Manildra Flour Mill will produce an extra 50 million kilograms of flour by July</li><li>Organic flour producers experience an influx of orders, as supermarket shelves are stripped bare</li><li>Home bakers turn to using oat flour and coconut flour alternatives </li></ul> </div>
</div></div><p>Manildra Flour Mill, based in New South Wales' Central West region, has experienced a 60 to 70 per cent increase in demand for its products in recent weeks.</p><p>Manager John Brunner said the company had been operating for 68 years and demand had reached a record high. </p><p>"It's been very hectic, and we've had to operate our factories all day and night to keep up with the demand, in the past couple of months," he said.</p><aside class="comp-rich-text-blockquote comp-embedded-float-full source-pullquote" >
<div class="quote">
<p>&quot;It's been even bigger than the lead up to Christmas or Easter, it's unprecedented.&quot;</p>
</div>
</aside><p>"The orders from supermarkets and hot bread shops have gone up as people stay at home to cook, rather than going out."</p><div class="view-embed-full clearfix" >
