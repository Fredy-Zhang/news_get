	<span class="cat-links"><span class="screen-reader-text">Posted in</span><a href="https://hongkongfp.com/category/hong-kong/" rel="category tag">Hong Kong</a></span>		<h1 class="entry-title ">
		Hongkongers’ support of pro-democracy shops ‘violates’ the free market, says Beijing	</h1>
	
	<div class="entry-subhead">
		<div class="entry-meta">
			<span class="author-avatar"><amp-img src="https://hongkongfp.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/Kelly-2-Copy-140x140.png" width="96" height="96" alt="Kelly Ho" class="avatar avatar-96 wp-user-avatar wp-user-avatar-96 alignnone photo amp-wp-enforced-sizes" layout="intrinsic"><noscript><img src="https://hongkongfp.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/Kelly-2-Copy-140x140.png" width="96" height="96" alt="Kelly Ho" class="avatar avatar-96 wp-user-avatar wp-user-avatar-96 alignnone photo"></noscript></amp-img></span><span class="byline"><span>by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="https://hongkongfp.com/author/kellyho/">Kelly Ho</a></span></span>			<span class="posted-on"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2020-05-04T14:31:00+08:00">4th May 2020</time><time class="updated" datetime="2020-05-04T14:44:58+08:00">4th May 2020</time></span>		</div><!-- .meta-info -->
			</div>

				<div class="main-content">

					
<article id="post-259794" class="post-259794 post type-post status-publish format-standard has-post-thumbnail hentry category-business category-health-eco category-hong-kong category-law-crime category-politics-protest tag-2020-coronavirus tag-australia tag-cheng-jingye tag-china-extradition tag-china-liaison-office tag-wuhan-virus tag-cy-leung tag-joshua-wong tag-marise-payne tag-scott-morrison entry">
	<div class="entry-content">
		
<p>Beijing’s office in Hong Kong has slammed shoppers’ support for pro-democracy businesses as “violating” free market principles.</p>



<p>In a statement <a rel="noreferrer noopener" href="http://www.locpg.gov.cn/jsdt/2020-05/02/c_1210601685.htm" target="_blank">issued</a> on Saturday, the office said “extreme radicals” had initiated illegal assemblies, disturbed shops and hurled petrol bombs during the May 1 holiday, despite demonstrations remaining low-key. It accused opposition lawmakers of using the “yellow economy” promotion to secure seats in the Legislative Council election in September, claiming that Hong Kong’s economy had been “kidnapped” by politics. </p>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><amp-img src="https://i2.wp.com/hongkongfp.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/20200501_165333-Copy.jpg?resize=960%2C540&amp;ssl=1" alt="hongkongers' golden week yellow shop queue &quot;May 1 2020&quot;" class="wp-image-259825 amp-wp-enforced-sizes" srcset="https://i2.wp.com/hongkongfp.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/20200501_165333-Copy.jpg?resize=1050%2C591&amp;ssl=1 1050w, https://i2.wp.com/hongkongfp.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/20200501_165333-Copy.jpg?resize=768%2C432&amp;ssl=1 768w, https://i2.wp.com/hongkongfp.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/20200501_165333-Copy.jpg?resize=706%2C397&amp;ssl=1 706w, https://i2.wp.com/hongkongfp.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/20200501_165333-Copy.jpg?w=1200&amp;ssl=1 1200w" width="960" height="540" layout="intrinsic"><noscript><img src="https://i2.wp.com/hongkongfp.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/20200501_165333-Copy.jpg?resize=960%2C540&amp;ssl=1" alt="hongkongers' golden week yellow shop queue &quot;May 1 2020&quot;" class="wp-image-259825" srcset="https://i2.wp.com/hongkongfp.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/20200501_165333-Copy.jpg?resize=1050%2C591&amp;ssl=1 1050w, https://i2.wp.com/hongkongfp.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/20200501_165333-Copy.jpg?resize=768%2C432&amp;ssl=1 768w, https://i2.wp.com/hongkongfp.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/20200501_165333-Copy.jpg?resize=706%2C397&amp;ssl=1 706w, https://i2.wp.com/hongkongfp.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/20200501_165333-Copy.jpg?w=1200&amp;ssl=1 1200w" sizes="(max-width: 960px) 100vw, 960px" width="960" height="540"></noscript></amp-img><figcaption>Citizens lined up outside a “yellow” shop in Causeway Bay on May 1. Photo: Rachel Wong/HKFP. </figcaption></figure>



<p>“Opposition lawmakers ignored the rules of the free market and made an utmost effort in hyping up the so-called ‘yellow economic circle’,” the statement read. </p><aside class="scaip scaip-1    "><aside id="newspack-ads-widget-5" class="widget_newspack-ads-widget clearfix"><div class="textwidget"><div id="div-gpt-amp-hkfp_content_336x280_1-5eb132e5d8b9e-336x280"><amp-ad width="336px" height="280px" type="doubleclick" data-slot="/82509819/hkfp_content_336x280_1"></amp-ad></div></div></aside></aside>



<p>“[They] manufacture a rift in society and unscrupulously disturb and destroy innocent shops. It is a kind of scorched-earth politics with politics kidnapping the economy,” the spokesperson added. </p>



<p>The liaison office’s remarks came after citizens responded to online calls to shop and dine at businesses that supported the anti-extradition bill movement which erupted last June. Organisers aimed at creating a “Hongkongers’ 5.1 Golden Week,” in response to the absence of mainland shoppers who usually arrive during the break. Tourism, however, has come to a halt amid coronavirus travel restrictions.</p>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><amp-img src="https://i0.wp.com/hongkongfp.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/49830489956_dd23cb366a_k-Copy.jpg?resize=960%2C640&amp;ssl=1" alt='"April 28 2020" IFC mall police cordon' class="wp-image-259492 amp-wp-enforced-sizes" srcset="https://i0.wp.com/hongkongfp.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/49830489956_dd23cb366a_k-Copy.jpg?resize=1050%2C700&amp;ssl=1 1050w, https://i0.wp.com/hongkongfp.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/49830489956_dd23cb366a_k-Copy.jpg?resize=768%2C512&amp;ssl=1 768w, https://i0.wp.com/hongkongfp.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/49830489956_dd23cb366a_k-Copy.jpg?resize=600%2C400&amp;ssl=1 600w, https://i0.wp.com/hongkongfp.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/49830489956_dd23cb366a_k-Copy.jpg?w=1080&amp;ssl=1 1080w" width="960" height="640" layout="intrinsic"><noscript><img src="https://i0.wp.com/hongkongfp.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/49830489956_dd23cb366a_k-Copy.jpg?resize=960%2C640&amp;ssl=1" alt='"April 28 2020" IFC mall police cordon' class="wp-image-259492" srcset="https://i0.wp.com/hongkongfp.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/49830489956_dd23cb366a_k-Copy.jpg?resize=1050%2C700&amp;ssl=1 1050w, https://i0.wp.com/hongkongfp.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/49830489956_dd23cb366a_k-Copy.jpg?resize=768%2C512&amp;ssl=1 768w, https://i0.wp.com/hongkongfp.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/49830489956_dd23cb366a_k-Copy.jpg?resize=600%2C400&amp;ssl=1 600w, https://i0.wp.com/hongkongfp.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/49830489956_dd23cb366a_k-Copy.jpg?w=1080&amp;ssl=1 1080w" sizes="(max-width: 960px) 100vw, 960px" width="960" height="640"></noscript></amp-img><figcaption>Police setting up a cordon inside the IFC mall. Photo: Studio Incendo. </figcaption></figure>



<p>According to the organisers’ Telegram channel, a total of 2,305 shops took part in the campaign, with an estimated turnover of more than HK$100 million. The organisers <a rel="noreferrer noopener" href="https://web.telegram.org/#/im?p=@HKGoldenWeek" target="_blank">encouraged</a> citizens to continue supporting pro-democracy shops after the campaign and promote the pro-democracy “yellow economy.”</p>



<p>City-wide protests failed to emerge on Labour Day last Friday, but riot police cleared Sha Tin’s New Town Plaza of protesters chanting slogans. Meanwhile, a 15-year-old boy was arrested over suspected arson in Prince Edward, where four people threw petrol bombs and rods at a police vehicle as it drove past the intersection of Nathan Road and Boundary Street.</p>



<h2>Free economy</h2>



<p>Hong Kong pro-democracy activist Joshua Wong wrote on Twitter that it was ironic that the Communist Party of China criticised Hongkongers as breaching the principles of the free market. He said citizens had the freedom to use their own money to support or denounce shops, adding that it was “precisely the fulfilment of the free market.” </p>



<figure class="wp-block-embed-twitter wp-block-embed is-type-rich is-provider-twitter"><div class="wp-block-embed__wrapper">

<p>Meanwhile, former chief executive Leung Chun-ying urged citizens to report on suspected violations of the “yellow” shops, such as breaching the Buildings Ordinance and Fire Services Ordinance. Leung said he opposed any form of violence, but blowing the whistle could be a legal means to “renovate” the pro-democracy shops, referring to the term “renovation” commonly used among protesters that meant thrashing anti-protest stores. </p>

<p>After 25 years in the top spot, Hong Kong was rated as the world’s second freest economy in the Heritage Foundation’s 2020 Index of Economic Freedom in March.</p>



<h2>Boycott warning against Australia </h2>



<p>Last Monday, China’s ambassador Cheng Jingye <a rel="noreferrer noopener" href="http://au.china-embassy.org/eng/sghdxwfb_1/t1773741.htm" target="_blank">warned</a> Australia that the country’s call for an independent inquiry into the origins and spread of the Covid-19 pandemic – which China opposed – could trigger a consumer boycott among Chinese students and tourists. Cheng said the Chinese public were “frustrated, dismayed and disappointed” with the calls. </p>



<p>“Maybe the ordinary people will think why they should drink Australian wine or eat Australian beef. Why couldn’t we do it differently?,” Cheng asked in an interview with Australian Financial Review.</p>

<p>Australian Prime Minister Scott Morrison <a rel="noreferrer noopener" href="https://www.pm.gov.au/media/press-conference-australian-parliament-house-act-1may20" target="_blank">defended</a> the suggestion for a probe as “entirely sensible,” and said it had “broad-based support,” while Foreign Minister Marise Payne <a rel="noreferrer noopener" href="https://www.foreignminister.gov.au/minister/marise-payne/transcript/interview-kim-landers-abc-am" target="_blank">rejected</a> Cheng’s warnings as “economic coercion.” </p>



<p>“It is important that we just have an objective, independent assessment of how this originated and learn the lessons from how that occurred,” Morrison told reporters in Canberra last Wednesday. </p>
