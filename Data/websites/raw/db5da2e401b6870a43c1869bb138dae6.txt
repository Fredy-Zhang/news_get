  <h1 class="page-title"><span class="field field-title">Egypt: Filmmaker jailed for directing song mocking Sisi dies in prison</span>
</h1>



  </div>

                
          </div>
                  
            <section class="page-main-content">
                                  <a id="main-content"></a>
                    
                <div class="field field-field-subhead">Shady Habash had been in prison for more than two years in pre-trial detention </div>
      
                      <div class="field field-field-main-image">  <img src="/sites/default/files/styles/article_page/public/images-story/ew_-bncxgai7ztq.jpeg?itok=8UvDE7ZY" width="1400" height="1543" alt="" typeof="foaf:Image" />


</div>
      
                    <div class="field field-field-cutline">Shady Habash, 24, was a film director and cinematographer (Instagram/@ShadyHabash)</div>
      
            <div class="author-field author-info author-location">
              <span class="author-by">By </span><div class="author-name"><a title="View user profile." href="/users/mee-staff" lang="" about="/users/mee-staff" typeof="schema:Person" property="schema:name" datatype="">MEE staff</a></div>          </div>
                  <div class="submitted-date">
          <span class="date-created">Published date:</span> 2 May 2020 12:26 UTC
            <span class="date-separator">| </span>
            <span class="date-updated">Last update:</span> 2 days 14 hours ago        </div>
            
                <div class="region region-content">
    <section id="block-meesocialshare" class="block block-mee-social-share block-mee-social-share-block clearfix">
  
    

      

<div  class="mee-social-share-block">
  <div class="sharethis-inline-share-buttons"></div>

</div>
  </section>

  <article data-history-node-id="169456" role="article" about="/news/egypt-filmmaker-jailed-directing-song-mocking-sisi-dies-prison" class="story full article-type clearfix">
                <div class="field field-body clearfix"><p>Egyptian film director and photographer Shady Habash&nbsp;died in Tora prison in the capital Cairo on Friday, according to human rights organisations.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Habash, 24, had been in prison since March 2018&nbsp;over directing&nbsp;a song mocking Egypt's President Abdel Fattah el-Sisi.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Ramy Essam, who performed the song, said Habash would be buried on Friday afternoon in Cairo.</p>
<p>"Shady Habash has died. Shady was the kindest and bravest of people. He never hurt anyone. May God have mercy on him," Essam said, mourning his friend&nbsp;Habash in a <a href="https://www.facebook.com/RamyEssamOfficial/posts/2974062472637718">Facebook</a> post.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Middle East Eye has not been able to verify independently the reason behind&nbsp;Habash's death, but a number of reports suggested that his death was avoidable.&nbsp;</p>
<p>"Shadi got very sick in his prison cell, his [fellow] inmates cried for help for some time, but guards and officers had not intervened until his last breath," said human rights advocate <a href="https://twitter.com/3yyash/status/1256400752328785924">Abdelrahman Ayyash</a>.&nbsp;</p>
<p>MEE reached out to the Egyptian government for comments but received no immediate response.&nbsp;<i>&nbsp;</i></p>
<h3>Jailed for a song</h3>
<p>Habash&nbsp;and his colleague Mustafa Gamal were arrested&nbsp;following the release of <em>Balaha</em>, a song that indirectly poked fun at Sisi, the former defence minister who came to power after a military coup ousted president Mohamed Morsi&nbsp;in 2013.</p>
<p>Essam, the singer who performed <em>Balaha</em>,&nbsp;is currently in exile in Sweden. The author of the song, <a href="https://www.middleeasteye.net/news/egyptian-poet-gets-three-years-prison-allegedly-insulting-soldiers">Galal el-Beheiry</a>, is also in jail.&nbsp;</p>
<p><div class="mee-readmore mee-readmore-article-story mee-readmore-type-image align-right">
  <a class="mee-readmore-img-link" href="/news/ramy-essam-and-ganzeer-continuing-egypts-revolution-exile"><img class="mee-readmore-image" src="/sites/default/files/styles/read_more/public/images-story/whatsapp_image_2020-01-26_at_18.38.19.jpeg?itok=AYTAQolh" width="400" height="250" alt="" typeof="foaf:Image" />

</a>  <div class="mee-readmore-description">Continuing Egypt's revolution from exile: Ramy Essam and Ganzeer</div>
    <a class="mee-readmore-link" href="/news/ramy-essam-and-ganzeer-continuing-egypts-revolution-exile">Read More »</a>
</div></p>
<p>"Balaha" is a derogatory nickname for Sisi, in reference to a character from a classic Egyptian movie known for being a compulsive liar.</p>
<p>"O Balaha, four years have finally passed in disgrace / With all your gang boys to that darkest jail, I wish you may rot in such a place," Essam <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FjBd_rvZr4U">sings</a>.</p>
<p>A statement by Essam after Habash's arrest said that the director "doesn’t have anything to do with the content and message of the song".</p>
<p>Charges brought against Habash and Gamal include&nbsp;membership of a "terrorist group," spreading false news, abuse of social media networks, blasphemy, contempt of religion and insulting the military.</p>
<p>They have both been in pre-trial detention pending investigations since their arrests.</p>
<p>Human Rights Watch has estimated that more than 60,000 political prisoners have been languishing in Egyptian jails since Sisi became president&nbsp;in 2014.&nbsp;</p>
<p>The former army general&nbsp;has routinely jailed critics, including secular and Muslim Brotherhood politicians, journalists, and human rights defenders. Hundreds have died in custody through medical negligence or other poor detention conditions.</p>
</div>
