    <h1 class="post-title">Outrage after India asks migrants to pay for train journey</h1>
    <p class="article-heading-des">Opposition Congress party offers to pay migrants' fare after government decision to make workers pay causes backlash.</p>
    <div class="article-heading-author-wrap">
      <div class="article-heading-author-img">
        <div class="article-heading-author-name">
          <div class="article-duration"><time class="timeagofunction" datetime="Mon May 04 2020 16:29:07 GMT+0000">04 May 2020 16:29 GMT</time></div>

</div></div></div></div></div></div><div></div></div></div></div><div><div><div><div style="width:100%"><div><div class="row article-main-content-wrapper"><div><div><div><div style="width:100%"><div><div class="col-md-8 article-main-content"><div><div style="border-style:none"><article id="main-article-block" class="main-article-block clear-fix"><div class="main-article-body"><figure class="main-article-mediaCaption"><div class="main-article-media"><img class="img-responsive main-article-media-img" src="/mritems/imagecache/mbdxxlarge/mritems/Images/2020/5/4/46f7e25c7f4645f7ac56a33deb4a77fb_18.jpg" alt="Migrant workers stranded in the western state of Gujarat wait to board a train in Ahmedabad [Amit Dave/Reuters]" title="Outrage after India asks migrants to pay for train journey"></div><figcaption class="main-article-caption">Migrant workers stranded in the western state of Gujarat wait to board a train in Ahmedabad [Amit Dave/Reuters]</figcaption></figure><div id="inpage-more-on-block" class="inpage-auto-embed hidden-xs"></div>
    <div class="article-p-wrapper" id="body-200771816342556199"><p class="speakable">The Indian government has been criticised for charging stranded migrants, travelling by special trains to their home states, for the journey.</p>
<p class="speakable">Millions of workers in the informal sector were rendered jobless after businesses across the country were closed following the implementation of a nationwide lockdown on March 25 in an attempt to contain the spread of the novel coronavirus.</p>
<h4>More:</h4>
<ul>
<li>
<h4><a href="https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2020/05/coronavirus-special-train-ferries-stranded-indian-migrants-200501110825223.html?utm_source=website&utm_medium=article_page&utm_campaign=read_more_links">Coronavirus: Special train ferries stranded Indian migrants </a></h4>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<h4><a href="https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2020/03/migrants-india-sprayed-disinfectant-fight-coronavirus-200330135052425.html?utm_source=website&utm_medium=article_page&utm_campaign=read_more_links">Migrants in India sprayed with disinfectant to fight coronavirus </a></h4>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<h4><a href="https://www.aljazeera.com/ajimpact/hungry-desperate-india-virus-controls-trap-migrant-workers-200402031123631.html?utm_source=website&utm_medium=article_page&utm_campaign=read_more_links">Hungry, desperate: India virus controls trap its migrant workers </a></h4>
</li>
</ul>
<p class="speakable">The first batch of migrants left the southern city of Hyderabad on May 1 for the eastern state of Jharkhand after the governmentÂ last week allowed special interstate trains and buses to transport those who wanted to return to their villages in other states.</p>
<p>But it emerged that migrants were charged as much as 800 Indian rupees ($10.58) for the journey. A daily wage worker earns between 200 rupees and 400 <span>rupeesÂ </span>($2.6-$5.3) a day.</p>
<p>Migrants were also asked to pay a surcharge of 50 rupees ($0.66) as buses run by the Karnataka government charged hefty ticket prices, according to reports in localÂ media.</p>
<h2>Congress party offers to pay fares</h2>
<p>On Monday, India's main opposition party Congress announced it will pay the migrants' fare after Indians took to social media criticising the government's move, with hashtags #PMCaresFund and #Railways becoming top Twitter trends.</p>
<p>"Our workers and labourers are the ambassadors of our nation's growth," said Congress president Sonia Gandhi in a statement.</p>
<p>"When our government can recognise its responsibility by arranging free air travel for our citizens stranded abroad, when the government can spend nearly Rs 100 crores [approximately $13m] on transport and food etc for just one public programme in Gujarat, when the Rail Ministry has the largesse to donate Rs 151 crores [<span>approximately $20m</span>] to the PM's Corona fund, then why can't these essential members of our nation's fabric be given a fraction of the same courtesy, especially free rail travel, at this hour of acute distress," the Congress chief said.</p>
<p>Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi has faced criticism for creating a new coronavirus relief fund, PM CARES, when about $500m was lying unspent in an older fund.</p>
<p>Top businesses and celebrities have pledged millions of dollars in new donations.</p>
<p>Many social media users asked why the government did not use the fund to help the poor and migrant workers.</p>
<p>Migrant workers have been forced to walk hundreds of kilometres, many of them without food, to reach their homes.</p>

<p>Senior leader and member of parliament from the governing Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) Subramanian Swamy demanded the government should pay for the fares.</p>
<p>"How moronic of the Government of India to charge steep rail fares from the half-starved migrant labourers! Indians stranded abroad were brought back free by Air India. If Railways refuse to budge then why not make PM CARES pay instead?"Â <span>he tweeted.</span></p>
<h2>Government reverses its decision</h2>
<p>Faced with mounting criticism, the office of Railway Minister Piyush Goyal said the government will pay 85 percent of the ticket prices and the remaining 15 percent will be borne by the state governments.</p>
<p>The government has also been criticised for using the air force to shower flower petals on front-line health workers instead of lending a helping hand to poor workers stuck in cities for at least a month now.</p>
<p>"The entire nation stands united in these challenging times," Defence Minister Rajnath Singh tweeted on Sunday.</p>
<p>On Saturday, more than 2,000 rural migrant workers stopped from returning home pelted police with stones in the western state of Gujarat, officialsÂ told the AFP news agency.<strong></strong></p>

<p>Gujarat is one of India's industrial hubs, and authorities there were bracing for a logistical "nightmare" after about two millionÂ migrant workers and their families signed up for permission to return home, a <span>stateÂ </span>official said.</p>
<p>In Indore, in the central state of Madhya Pradesh, 14 migrant workers and four others were found by police on Saturday crammed into a cement mixer, local media reported. The migrants had been trying to return home from the western Maharashtra state to the northern state of Uttar Pradesh - a 1,200-kilometre (745-mile) journey.</p>
<p>In an exodus, many migrants already managed to return to their villages, mostly on foot. But local media reported that some died on their long journeys. Many others have been stranded at crowded shelters in various cities.</p></div>
