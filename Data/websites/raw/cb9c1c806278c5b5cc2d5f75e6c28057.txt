<h2>Players Claim Bans Last Until 2029</h2>
<div class="region region-content">
<div data-drupal-messages-fallback class="hidden"></div><div id="block-techraptor-content" class="block block-system block-system-main-block">
<article data-history-node-id="21873" role="article" about="/gaming/news/mount-blade-2-bannerlord-bans-allegedly-given-for-violating-chinese-law" class="node node--type-article node--view-mode-full">
<div class="node__content">
<div class="clearfix text-formatted field field--name-body field--type-text-with-summary field--label-hidden field__item"><p>Several Asian players are alleging that <em><a href="https://techraptor.net/game/mount-blade-ii-bannerlord">Mount &amp; Blade 2: Bannerlord</a> </em>bans are being handed out by a Chinese administrator for people who are violating Chinese law. These alleged violations include political speech such as "Free Hong Kong" and more innocuous (but equally banned) words like "coronavirus," and the allegations only get worse from there.</p>
<p>These allegations were made in two separate locations that we've found thus far. The first comes from TaleWorlds own forums in <a href="https://forums.taleworlds.com/index.php?threads/the-east-asian-servers-are-hosted-by-a-chinese-forum-this-forum-is-telling-its-chinese-users-to-report-any-anti-prc-discussion-on-ea-servers.421427/">a topic</a> that is now locked. This discussion opens by alleging that any "anti-PRC" (People's Republic of China) discussion should be reported, in regards to the East Asian servers, which are allegedly moderated by Chinese administrators, although it is not believed the servers are hosted in China.</p><div data-embed-button="embed_block" data-entity-embed-display="view_mode:block_content.full" data-entity-type="block_content" data-entity-uuid="dbe4aee2-5f7b-4222-94bc-c64dee4d47cf" id="dbe4aee2-5f7b-4222-94bc-c64dee4d47cf" data-langcode="en" class="align-center embedded-entity"> <div class="layout layout--onecol">
<div class="layout__region layout__region--content">

</div>

</div>
<p>One player is said to be reported simply for saying "Coronavirus". Other mentioned incidents include using the player name "#HongKong." Furthermore, some people are reporting that players are being banned without warning.</p>
<p>Similarly, a <a href="https://www.reddit.com/r/MB2Bannerlord/comments/gd4y8n/warning_dont_play_with_china_or_you_may_be_banned/">submission</a> to the /r/MB2Bannerlord subreddit warns players not to "play with china", presumably meaning to avoid playing with Chinese players. This submission further alleges that admins have the right to ban people on official servers for violating Chinese law and that these bans will last until 2029.</p><div data-embed-button="embed_block" data-entity-embed-display="view_mode:block_content.full" data-entity-type="block_content" data-entity-uuid="a38dd238-a015-4198-8af4-7b369090353a" id="a38dd238-a015-4198-8af4-7b369090353a" data-langcode="en" class="align-center embedded-entity"> <div class="layout layout--onecol">

<p>The Reddit submission on the <em>Mount &amp; Blade 2: Bannerlord </em>bans notes that one player got banned for "inappropriate behavior" for having the name 国武汉共产党肺炎, which they say translates to "China Wuhan CCP virus." The OP claims that this ban authority is in force on all servers.​</p>

<h2>What is TaleWorlds Doing About These Alleged<em> Mount &amp; Blade 2: Bannerlord</em> Bans?</h2>
<p>What is TaleWorlds doing about these alleged <em>Mount &amp; Blade 2: Bannerlord</em> bans in light of this new information? We don't have too much information just yet, but it looks like they're aware of the situation.</p>
<p>"[As far as I know,] the issue is something that the team is aware [of] and we'll probably get a proper statement soon," stated TaleWorlds section moderator IceNoVa on the game's official forums. "[Either way,] thank you for bringing this to attention."</p>
<p>We've reached out to TaleWorlds and will update this article when we receive a reply.
